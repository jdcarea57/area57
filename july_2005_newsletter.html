Newsletter - July 2005
 

There have been various discussions lately with regard to the fact that we don’t go to as many car events en masse as we used to in the earlier days of Area 57. The reasons for this involve several factors, and I believe it has to do with the way people’s lives and interests change over the years. To a certain extent the classic car scene has changed; with a few exceptions car shows in general, as well as Jaguar ones, do not attract the numbers that they once did. Many of the JDC events return to the same venues year after year and, much as we are all enthusiastic about the marque, there are only so many times that you want to see row after row of Jaguars. In the past, one of the most popular shows (which on occasion had about fifteen of our cars all arriving together) was the Police Show at Chigwell. This really was a show that had something for everyone, but sadly it too has declined in the past few years and it is two or three years since we attended as a club. As I said earlier, people’s interests do change and whereas at one time, many of us were quite happy to go to an event every Sunday, not many are now able to do so, whether by choice or circumstances.

If you know of a show that from past experience is worth attending, or a place of interest that would be worth organising a visit to, please let Alan or myself know well in advance. This can then be discussed at a meeting as well as being reported in the newsletter, and meeting places etc. can be organised. Not long ago we raised the possibility of a visit to the Shuttleworth Collection, but there wasn’t enough interest to follow it up. It did at least, however, get discussed rather than just appearing as a mention in the Newsletter. In addition, people inevitably have holiday or other commitments which can also rule out the type of mass attendance that used to occur in the past.

Having said all that, the Area remains a very strong social group. This is borne out by the success of the Breakfast Runs, Christmas Dinners, barbecues etc. Outings such as the visit to the De Havilland Museum, Standon Museum and the trip on the "Waverly" are all indications of the strength of the club in ways other than going to car shows, (although the excellent attendance each year at the Enfield Pageant of Motoring would certainly be the envy of many other clubs).

It is worth noting that the Area will reach its twentieth anniversary next March. We are the only UK JDC Area that holds twice monthly meetings, and we are still one of only four JDC Areas which operates its own web site. These, along with the production & distribution of the monthly newsletter, would hardly seem to be the mark of a club in decline.

Last month, I should have mentioned a show at Althorne, near Burnham on Crouch. It is in aid of the Little Haven Children’s Hospice. The £40 that the sale of the floral pots that the Clockhouse Nursery donated for our Pageant Stand is to be given to this charity. The centre that Martin Rogers attends is to close, so we were not able to give the money to Dick and Eileen as usual. This show takes place on Sunday, 21st August and Dick may be able to tell you more about it as I don’t have a leaflet.

Hopefully we haven’t left it too late to squeeze in an extra Breakfast Run and Len and Sally are making enquiries at the Woodman, Nuthampstead, near Royston. We have been told that the landlord will accommodate a breakfast group, so it remains to be seen whether anything can be fixed for September 4th. Deciding on this date was a typical example of how difficult it can be to sort out a date to suit the majority of people.

Doug and I were chatting at Hoddesdon to a couple from the M.G. Club that meet every Monday at the Botany Bay Cricket Club, just off the Enfield Ridgeway. They said we would be welcome anytime, so maybe it might be worth thinking about meeting at the Legion as usual, one Monday, and then driving up to visit them in convoy.

We had about ten cars at the Hoddesdon Show on 9th July, but unfortunately no sunshine until just before we left to enjoy tea and cakes with Audrey and Keith. The show does attract a wide variety of cars, although again, numbers as a whole seem to be lower than they used to be.

At some point in the proceedings we returned to our car to find a FOR SALE sign saying it had plastic body, Cortina parts, Morris Minor engine etc. Price £800 and a phone number. Correctly assuming Eric to be to blame we put it on his XK with the additional line "Insanity forces sale". Throughout the day we were all amazed at how many people kept tapping the body and some people were even seen ringing the phone number!

I believe several cars went along to The Hollybush for Dick’s SS meeting recently and had a pleasant evening, and the Ace Cafe Classic Car Evenings on the 2nd Tuesday of every month are now attracting a few regulars.

Just a reminder about Keith’s 60th Birthday Bash (now with live music) on 6th August. I’m going to quote from an invitation Doug and I received recently; "Your presence not your presents" is what Keith wants.

XJ DAY, OLD WARDEN - 26th July

Report by Graham & Karen Smart

Although we had had very good weather, this particular Sunday was overcast, the sun not appearing till 1pm.

There was a good mix of cars but unsurprisingly, it being the 30th anniversary, a lot of XJSs, some really nice cabriolets among them. Several E-types were in attendance including Len & Sallys V12. In the concours, the bronze class was won by a very smart XJ40 in solent blue, but just to show what the competion was like, in third place was a one owner, 20,000 mile red XJS which looked a winner to me but in fact scored only 647 points out of a 1000! I had to leave at 3pm, (working again), so I did not hear the other results.

Entry was £15 per car, but that did include a visit to the Shuttleworth collection, a museum of aircraft from both world wars plus some old cars and carriages. The XJ register were selling hats, t-shirts, badges etc., but there was only one stall selling spares, a little disappointing, but overall not a bad day.
