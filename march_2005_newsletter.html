Newsletter - March 2005
 

Monday 21st March - Minutes of the 19th A.G.M

Twenty three members were present at the A.G.M., just one less than last year. Alan Collins as Area Rep thanked everyone for coming and it was agreed that the Minutes of last year’s meeting should be taken as read.

Alan gave us a quick reminder of some of the events and social occasions that various members went to throughout the year. The Enfield Pageant was very successful with the area winning the first prize of £250 for Best Club Stand. Alan thanked Eric Tetsell for his organisation, John Wilkinson and Keith Oswick for mowing the plot and providing floral decoration, plus any girls involved in cooking duties. As far as car events are concerned, the main ones that need mentioning are Harpenden and Croxley Green, with the former attracting 2000 vehicles. Other places visited were the Maldon Museum of Power, Redbridge, Hoddesdon, Buntingford, Trent Park, Shendish Manor, Nazeing and Shenley. The Ace Cafe has become a regular venue for Dick Rogers and Eric Tetsell, and Keith & Audrey Williams have enjoyed a couple of weekends organised by the EDVVS. There were two XK weekends, one at Hailsham, the other at Stamford. The September Area weekend at Corse Lawn near Tewkesbury was very successful. Thanks to Dick and Eric for organising the visit to the Standon Transport Museum and the pub lunch nearby. Another very enjoyable outing was the trip to Southend on the paddle steamer "Waverly"; our thanks to Keith O for organising this one for us.

We had three Breakfast Runs this year; the two regular ones to Rickling Green plus a summer one (when it rained) to the Red House near Royston. For the second year we enjoyed a Christmas Dinner at the Old Manor, Potters Bar.

Alan said that he was pleased to see that Doug, Ron, Pam, Brian and Karen are all healthy again after having hospital treatment for various problems. He closed his Area Rep’s report by thanking everyone for their support and friendship which keeps the Area going.

Barbara Collins circulated the statement of Accounts which shows a continued healthy balance which has benefited from our £250 prize at the Pageant.

Wendy Shipman thanked Roy & Kim Fludgate for their continued production of the Newsletter, whether you receive it by post or E-mail. She also thanked Phil Kendon for putting her contributions to the Jaguar Driver magazine. As usual she requested more contributions from members and was appreciative of those who do submit articles.

Roy Fludgate told us that three memberships have lapsed but we have gained two, and currently we have forty one memberships, representing sixty people and sixty eight vehicles. He reminded members that the Area website is updated regularly so check for updates to the club calendar during the month, rather than waiting for the Newsletter.

There were no volunteers for any of the official posts and Alan and Barbara Collins, Wendy Shipman and Roy Fludgate have all agreed to continue as before. Phil Kendon will continue as Club Archivist but other commitments mean that he cannot always attend venues so is grateful for photos (with venue and date, please).

There was one item for discussion under Any Other Business. It had been agreed last year that any memberships received after the end of February would incur a £5 rejoining fee as by this time, Barbara has banked subscriptions and Roy compiled membership lists, so considerable inconvenience is caused by late renewals. Despite plenty of reminders, one member sent in a late renewal and was annoyed to have his cheque returned with a request for the extra £5. This was fully discussed as the renewal was only a few days late but a vote of twenty in favour, and three against made it clear that the membership felt that the rule should be upheld.

It was then time for Valerie Dyall to present the John Dyall Trophy for service to the Club throughout the year. The recipient this year was Dick Rogers. Well deserved.

Usually, founder member and first Area Rep. Chris Engeham proposes a vote of thanks to Alan and team but he was unable to attend this year, however he did send a letter which was passed around at the meeting, and is also reproduced below.

Letter from Chris Engeham:

Dear Alan & Barbara

Another AGM, doesn't time fly these days.

As you know I am not currently active in any Jaguar activities, no longer owning any Jaguar cars, but still maintain very fond interest in all classic Jaguar matters, the JDC and Area 57. It gives me great pleasure to know that there are so many members still enjoying their cars and the social side that has always been second to none at "57".

In case I can't get to the meeting next Monday can you pass on my warmest regards to all my friends, including yourselves of course, and wish Area 57 another exciting motoring year.

Keep up the good work Alan and many thanks to you, Barbara, Wendy and Roy and the many others that contribute to the running and keeping alive the club that has given me and so many others a lot of very good times and memories, and all done without the usual club politics and in fighting that you hear of in other clubs. I think Brian K summed it up well when he said it is like having an extra family around you.

With very best regards

Chris

Alan then re-iterated his earlier thanks to everyone for their support and friendship, and the A.G.M. was then closed.

With the A.G.M. concluded we then began the usual meeting, which mainly consisted of new dates for the calendar.

The Luton Festival of Transport, which Eric has visited in the past and can recommend is on 12th June.

The Classics on the Common meeting at Harpenden is on Wednesday 27th July and as last year, is only open to pre-booked vehicles.

There are two events at Croxley Green; Wednesday 3rd August and Sunday 18th September, although the first of these dates clashes with a Jaguar evening at the Ace Cafe.

I have been sent three dates from the Broxbourne Council; Rock ‘n Roll Day at Waltham Cross on Saturday 11th June. The same venue on Saturday 6th August is Back to the 60’s. The usual Vintage and Classic Car display at Hoddesdon is on Saturday 9th July. Details of any of these events are available from Karen Thurgood (01992-785555 ext 5716).

Barbara has started collecting names and money for the first of our Breakfast Runs on Sunday 24th April.

Hopefully at the next meeting it will be decided whether there is enough interest in visiting the Shuttleworth Collection for their Military Pageant Display on 5th June. A reminder that they are unable to give a group reduction on price, so the normal £15 applies which would have to be paid two or three weeks in advance. If you are interested but won’t be at next month’s meeting, give either Alan or myself a ring.

It has been proposed that we could hold the Christmas Dinner at the Pearse House Hotel, Bishops Stortford. This is where Alan works, and we would be able to have a room to ourselves for the evening. The only Saturdays in December available are the 3rd or the 17th. Alan would like to get a rapid decision in order to place the booking.
