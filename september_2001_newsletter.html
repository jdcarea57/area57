Newsletter - September 2001

STEAM AND COUNTRY SHOW - Holly Hill Farm, Enfield - Monday 27th August 2001

The first joint event held by the well established Heavy Horse Show and the Whitewebb Museum’s Steam and Country Fair, previously held at Trent Park, seems to have been very successful. Unfortunately, for those attending this two day event on the Sunday, it was very wet and miserable. On Bank Holiday Monday however, it was just about perfect. The EDVVS had asked us to put on a club display and about fifteen of our cars made a good show. Thanks to Eric for organising the passes again and to John W. for mowing our area for us. We treated this as a mini Pageant, with our tent and cookers, so that Barbara and I could do the bacon rolls again.

Dave Norton did his usual good job of commentating on all the cars as we went into the arena. The tractors and steam vehicles were also described by Dave, while a gentleman answering to the name of Jumbo did the honors for the Heavy Horse Association. To the uninitiated it was interesting to hear the difference between the various horses and their wagons, cars and harness etc. One highlight was an old Shand Mason fire engine pulled by two grey Percherons. They galloped around the arena before extinguishing a burning hay bale. Several other horses were being used to demonstrate the way old fashioned farm implements were pulled across the fields, in contrast to a massive John Deere tractor.

The mix of cars, steam vehicles and horses worked well making an enjoyable, relaxing show which hopefully will become a regular event.

CHIGWELL SHOW - Sunday 2nd September

As a club, we have been going to the MG and Police Show at Chigwell for many years and for a few weeks we expected last year’s show to be the last because of various changes in the Met and Essex Police areas. I think for only the second time, all the cars were at the Roding end of the show-ground, which does give the feeling of not quite being part of the rest of the show. Mick and Jacquie had arrived first and managed to secure an area for us but we were not able to spread ourselves too much. I think the general opinion is that this show is not what it was. As we were not given arena programmes this year I don’t know what events were scheduled, but it did not seem as though the normal motor bike competition and horse and dog displays were put on the by the police. There was a dog handling demonstration by a dog club and a different motor bike display team. Frank Bruno made an appearance to sign autographs. The highlight of this show for the past few years seems to have become Area 57’s invasion of the dodgems; about ten cars this year for three turns. At the time of writing, about ten days later, I still have the bruises to prove that a good time was had by all.

For a few years now, someone from the M.G. club has chosen one of our cars as best Jaguar, and we were pleased to get a little shield when our XK was picked. However, Phil and Heather went one better as their MK VII was chosen as Classic Car of the Show and they have a silver salver for a year.

POLICE P.R. TACTICS FAIL TO IMPRESS

On September 1st and 2nd, the Chigwell Police Club staged its annual summer fayre. This event is always an enjoyable public relations exercise. I was, however, disappointed with the fact that Essex Police did not participate, not even by providing a constable to assist with vehicle access to the club from the High Road. Instead, Essex Police staged their own version of PR, that of Positive Revenue, by operating a speed trap in a lay-by between the top of Chigwell High Road and Rolls Park Corner on Sunday evening. It is quite apparent that Essex Police do not realise that the public are aware of the financial gains being exploited by Essex Police to the detriment of other services. The constabulary overtime analysis would make interesting reading. Speed traps are extremely useful near schools and accident blackspots as a deterrent but not as a license to print money.

WAREHAM WEEKEND

Over the 7th-9th September, fourteen of us spent the weekend at the Springfield Hotel near Wareham in Dorset; a return visit for some of us. When Eric and Margot, Dick and Eileen and ourselves arrived about 3 p.m, we found Len and Sally, Keith and Audrey and Tom and Enid already sitting in the garden with several bottles of wine on the table. A little later, the group was completed by the arrival of Phil and Heather. The hotel has both indoor and outdoor swimming pools but the weather was so kind to us that it was the outside one which was used. Three full sized snooker tables were among the other facilities at the hotel and Eileen was the champion at potting the cue ball!

Saturday morning started with drizzle but it all cleared by about 10.30 a.m and it was decided to take a trip on a preserved railway (full-size) which ran between a village near Corfe Castle and Swanage. I might do the hotel bookings but I take no credit for the fortuitous way in which things worked out that day. It turned out to be a special steam weekend with extra engines in steam hauling both passenger and goods wagons all day, including some working as double headers. At a station half way along the line, there was a small classic car show, so we all got off there for a look round. I know there is probably some prejudice but the best car was a red MK II Jaguar. Back on the train for Swanage and while looking out to sea in glorious sunshine, we saw lots of people wandering around in various costumes, Morris Dancers etc. We headed back towards the station only to find we had just met the beginning of a parade of all the various groups, dancing, playing instruments etc.; all very colourful. It turned out to be an annual folk festival with groups from all over the county.

On Sunday morning, Eric and Margot left after breakfast as they were driving to Dover for a few days in France. The rest of us drove to the local beauty spot of Lulworth Cave where ten of us had a boat ride along the coast to Dundle Door and back. After that, Tom and Enid and Keith and Audrey headed home while the rest of us visited Monkey World, an ape rescue centre, a few miles away. Apparently they have been housing all sorts of monkeys, chimps, orangutans, lemurs etc. for about fifteen years. Most of them have been rescued from the illegal pet trade or from laboratories. The conditions for them are excellent.

For the last few years, we have spent the evenings on various quizzes that some of us have organised and it makes an enjoyable after dinner pastime. With a lot of help from the weather it had been another very enjoyable weekend.

Just a very brief mention of other events that people have attended recently. Apparently, National Day at Gaydon was very wet according to Chris Engeham, as was Area 33’s day at Audley End where it appears that the entry system could have been better.

According to Alan Collins, the show at St Mary's School, Bishops Stortford is growing, but those members who attended felt they needed some practise at putting up the club banner and flags, I gather.
