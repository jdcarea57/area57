Newsletter - April 2002

The past few Sundays have enjoyed pretty good weather, and the morning of the first Breakfast Run to the Cricketers at Rickling Green was no exception. This very popular event seems to have attracted record numbers this year, and the convoy of Jaguars leaving Bullsmoor Lane in Enfield at 8 o’clock was longer than it has been for some time. We arrived at the pub shortly before the other group, who had started from the Royal Forest Hotel in Chingford, and after fifteen minutes or so chatting in the morning sunshine we all went in for the usual hearty full English breakfast, provided by the staff at the Cricketers with their usual efficiency.

After we had eaten our fill, several of us went back to Alan & Barbara’s house for tea, coffee, and cake, and the day remained so pleasant that Alan decided at around 1 p.m. that it would be a good idea to get some suitable food in and light up the barbecue. Many thanks Alan & Barbara for what turned out to be a whole day out. (For those of you who nipped off earlier, you missed an excellent afternoon).

Our next club meeting is on Bank Holiday Monday, 6th May, the same day as the charity show at Stanborough Lakes, Welwyn Garden City. As usual we will meet up in the car park of the Gosling Sports Stadium first, where Barbara will collect £5 per car. I imagine that everybody knows how to get there by now but if anyone needs directions, just give me a ring. Also, if anyone wants to meet at our house at 9.30, please let me know.

Although the Pageant is a week later this year to coincide with the Jubilee Bank Holiday, the weeks are flying by. Please contact Eric Tetsell as soon as possible if you can show your car on Sunday 2nd June and/or Monday 3rd. Don’t forget that although we only put on a club display on those two days, it is open on Saturday so anybody wanting to go over there can put their car there on our plot.

I imagine that the May Newsletter will be with you before the Pageant but just in case it’s not, I’ll do some reminders now. First and foremost please be on site by 9 a.m. The membership list which you all have, lists plenty of mobile numbers so it you are held up, let somebody know! Everyone knows by now, what to bring in the way of water, cookers, tables etc. so I’ll just remind everyone to bring a mug. Of course the day will start with our usual bacon rolls.

By the way, if anyone needs a new T-shirt, sweater etc. before then, please let me know.

You may remember that last June, Dick Rogers made arrangements with Nick Drukker for the SS Register to visit Whitewebbs Museum. Despite a beautiful day, only Nick and one other SS turned up (apart from Dick’s own car). Many Museum cars went to another event and it also clashed with a charity event at Chigwell that some of our members attended. Nick was very impressed with the Museum and wrote an article to that effect in the Jaguar Driver a few months ago. He hopes that by giving his Register members more warning, they can do better this year and he has already mentioned in the Jaguar Driver, a repeat visit to the Museum on 29th Sept. which he has arranged with Dick.

Brian Kingsley has a list of what are presumably Sunday lunch time meetings at various Hertfordshire pubs. If you are interested, speak to Brian at a meeting, or phone him.

WHITEWEBBS MUSEUM - Isle of Wight Ramble - April 2002

Report by Audrey Williams

Friday 5th April was a beautiful warm Spring day. We reached our hotel in plenty of time to explore Old Shanklin Village and sit outside for a cream tea. Whitewebbs made us very welcome and so Friday evening was quite a jolly affair, partly due to the concessions on House wine and the marvelous magician who entertained us during dinner.

Saturday 6th April was a beautiful cold, Spring day. We all parked at Havenstreet for the day to go on the Steam Railway. The journey to Smallbrook was wonderful with rampant wild flowers on both sides of the banks. The journey to Ryde was not so wonderful.. It became cold and windy and our main problem was finding a decent pub for lunch We found shelter in the King Lud only to find several other ramblers had had the same idea. Ryde was disappointing - all tacky souvenir shops and charity shops!! When we got back to Havenstreet there was a classic double decker bus waiting for us. It took us through leafy lanes to Newpark where we visited the Classic Boat Museum and the Bus Museum. Entertainment at dinner was noisy!! Our table was next to the music and so we all had to shout to make ourselves heard! The raffle made up for this, Stan really excelled himself - not an old can of WD40 or a packet of biscuits in sight!! He collected £200 which will be handed over to the Cancer fund at Stanborough Lakes on the Bank Holiday Monday.

Sunday morning was planned for us as a trip to the Pearl factory on the coast road back to the ferry at Yarmouth. All the ladies were given a free gift (some pretty earrings and a not so pretty brooch). I half thought of giving mine to Stan for the next raffle but changed by mind when I saw some ladies wearing their brooches at lunchtime!! I didn’t want to offend anyone as it was our first trip with the museum. ( I’ll wait until next year).

All 98 of us stopped off at Lymington for lunch at Towles Restaurant. It’s not far from Beaulieu , so if you are ever in that area - it’s a must. The Sweets in particular were amazing with over 15 to choose from.

All in all it was a super weekend and excellent value for money - most of the trips and meals were included in the price. We hope to go on the Ramble again next year and may take advantage of the extra day - got to be worth the extra £35!!

ACADEMIC WORKS INSIDE JAGUAR TEAM ON SAFETY PROJECT

Privileged purchases of the latest model from Jaguar will benefit from University of Hertfordshire expertise. Academic William Tiu worked on the X400 safety technology during a sabbatical period with the manufacturer at Coventry. As part of the company’s Engineering Research Centre Group. Dr. Tiu, a senior lecturer in the faculty of Engineering and Information Sciences, worked side-by-side with specialist studying safety aspects of occupants in the event of a crash.

"I was a member of a six-strong team," said Dr. Tiu. "The excellent thing about this kind of sabbatical is that both parties - the university and Jaguar - gain from the association. It was certainly very good experience for me and I have gained tremendous insight through the work."

Much of the project entailed computer-aided simulation of factors involved in accident scenarios in which the £30,000 V6 3 litre vehicle might be engaged. He underlined the daunting speed wiith which an impact happens once contact is made with another object. "The duration is just one tenth of a second." Much of his activity at Jaguar was the examination of how the lower part of the anatomy is affected during that brief shock, looking at the way the lower abdomen and the legs interact with the floor pan. "We modelled the way that the deformation of the pan affects the feet and the legs", he added. "Frequently the accident victim experiences life-threatening loss of blood from the vessels in the lower anatomy and the legs having survived the crash."

In the past four years, he said, the European Crash Regulatory Body had been paying much more attention to effects of impacts upon the lower part of the body. "One could say that with air bag technology so well advanced, the top of the body has been dealt with, which leaves the matter of the bottom half."
