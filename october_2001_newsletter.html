Newsletter - October 2001
 
I don’t know whether many of you remember The Fall and Rise of Reginald Perrin on TV, in which Geoffrey Palmer regularly had a "bit of a cock up on the catering front". That is how our Christmas Dinner plans can be described. Despite booking at the George IV, (where the event has been held for the past two years), back in June, Barbara was recently informed that they had mistakenly booked us in for lunch not dinner on 8th December. After several phone calls, however, we have found an alternative; the Plough in Cattlegate Road, Enfield. The date remains the same, and we will have their conservatory restaurant to ourselves.  Car parking will be easier than at Amwell, and this will be the first time that we have held the Area Christmas Dinner in Enfield.
 
At the end of September, the Cancer Research Show was held at a new venue; The Royal Oak in Stapleford Abbots. Despite a very wet day, I understand quite a few of our cars braved the elements and Len and Sally Smerald's E-type was chosen by one of the "London’s Burning" cast as his favourite car. Gwynith has told me they raised about £700 and her letter of thanks follows.
 
Buckhurst Hill Friends of the Imperial Cancer Research Fund - Veteran, Vintage & Classic Car Meeting held at The Royal Oak, Stapleford Abbotts, Essex, - Sunday 30th September 2001
 
Dear Friends,
A very special thanks to those who attended the above event in such dreadful weather.  Also I say to you all a very big thanks from our Group - we are most grateful. DE HAVILLAND AIRCRAFT HERITAGE CENTRE - Sunday 14th October 2001
 
About two dozen of us visited the de Havilland Aircraft Heritage Centre at London Colney, where we were allocated our own parking area outside the main hangar. We were made very welcome by Reg Davey who had been an observer on various types of aircraft during the war. He gave us a history of the development of the wood construction high speed unarmed bomber which became known as the Mosquito. Although I can tell the difference between a Tiger Moth and a Spitfire and a Jumbo Jet from Concord, my aviation knowledge is extremely limited, to say the least, so I can’t tell you much about the various de Havilland planes and memorabilia on display. Several of the "boys" took the opportunity to scramble up into a Mosquito and found the thought of spending up to eight hours in such cramped conditions with the noise and vibration of the engines, pretty awful. The Museum is staffed entirely by volunteers who give up their spare time to help renovate aircraft, engines etc. as well as showing people round. They hope to develop an education centre and eventually apply for lottery funding.
 
After a couple of hours, most people came back to our house for tea and cakes. It was a bit of a squash, but we just about managed to fit everyone in!
