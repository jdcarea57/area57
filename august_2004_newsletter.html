Newsletter - August 2004

Outside many towns you can "Park and Ride"; at Alan and Barbara’s on 15th August it was more a case of "Eat and Ride". This was the third time that we have had one of these gatherings to enable people to have rides in cars they have not been in before. Alan did a great job with all the barbecue cooking while Barbara had laid on a good selection of salads plus potatoes. Once the important business of eating was mainly over, the rides started. Wayne brought his Rover 100 and Richard came in his 4 1/2 litre Bentley which added to the variety. The age range as far as Jaguars was concerned spread from Doug’s XK120 to Steve Whitford’s brand new X-type diesel. The weather was ideal, so it was another enjoyable get together, (apart from the wasp).

The club has received a letter of thanks from the Formont Centre, which Martin Rogers attends, for the donation we made after some of our members bought the planted pots that the Clock House Nursery had given us for our display at the Pageant.

Audrey apologised to me for sending her reports of Harpenden and Croxley Green direct to Roy by E-mail. If anybody finds this easier than writing out something and sending it to me, then please do so. I am just pleased to get copy for the Newsletter, however it gets there. (From our point of view it makes life easier....less typing to do. Kim & Roy)

SIZZLING SUMMER SPECTACULARS

HARPENDEN COMMON - WED JULY 28th & CROXLEY GREEN - WED AUG 4th

Reported by Audrey & Keith Williams

Harpenden:

We (Brian, Ron, Richard & ourselves) arrived in convoy at 4pm precisely, having been given strict instructions not to get there any earlier as there would be no admittance. The first show area was already almost full, and we were lucky to get one of the last pitches. As always there was a terrific range of cars, including a number of magnificent Tatras. (I understand some 3,000 vehicles had pre-booked !) We were somewhat limited in the number of cars we saw as it was a bit of a social gathering for us; we had several visitors calling in to see us (people we once worked with!!).

Tradition has it that the evening ends for us with a quiet walk into Harpenden itself, to a wonderful fish n’ chip shop (9/10 for brilliant crispy chips).

Croxley Green:

Another hot evening & we arranged to meet a friend who lived locally in the Church Langley Cricket Club, and then travel cross country to the Green. Unfortunately he had problems with his Jag. and phoned us to say he hoped to get along later, but never actually made it. We were surprised when we got there to see Ron and Brian had arrived before us, and luckily had saved us a nice space. Not such a good turn out as last year but still a nice assortment of cars including three Deal Bugattis and a lovely 1935 Alvis saloon. Richard arrived in his Bentley which attracted a lot of attention as usual, but Eric and his Team Escargot, with their stretched Limo (although not a classic) was a real crowd-puller! Chip van on site this year but only 6/10 as they were a bit soggy and not too hot. No loos at all !!

Two wonderful balmy summer Wednesday evenings.

Thank you for your congratulations message for Laura. She’s off to Cambridge in September for a year – so we’ve both got to keep working !
