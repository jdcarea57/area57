Newsletter - July 2003

Eight Club members joined Tom Jones and his family in a packed church at Chingford for the funeral of his wife, Enid. At the family’s request the Club did not send flowers, but a donation to Cancer UK has been sent on the club’s behalf. A letter from Tom is shown later in this newsletter, and I know I speak for everyone when I say that we look forward to his return to club meetings when he is sufficiently mobile after his hip replacement.

We may be in the middle of summer but we have recently booked our Christmas Dinner; a change of venue this time to the Old Manor at Potters Bar. Despite this being almost on our doorstep, we have never eaten there, but Keith Oswick knew they had a separate room that could take our numbers. He and Dick Rogers took a drive from a club meeting back to Potters Bar and booked it straight away. The date is December 13th.

The Hoddesdon Show on 12th July was a glorious day; nearly every year has been sunny for this event. The number of cars we had was lower this year but it is always a pleasant relaxing day. Keith and Audrey Williams always extend an invitation for tea and cakes afterwards but this year only Brian Kingsley and ourselves were able to take up the offer.

The Classics on the Common meeting at Harpenden is still attracting more and more vehicles; around 2,000 this year (including an XJR9 in Silk Cut racing livery) with a similar number turned away. Considering this is held on a Wednesday evening, the variety of vehicles, ranging from the aforementioned XJR9 through every type of classic car and motorcycle and even an old double deck bus, is nothing short of amazing. This is really a meeting for petrolheads with, this year, even the main road lined with spectators all night just watching the vehicles drive by!

VISIT TO SS REGISTER MEETING

Report by Dick Rogers

On Monday 9th June, I went to the meeting of the SS jaguars on a lovely evening, having met up with Keith and Audrey, Brian K. and Richard Freestone at Chiswell Green just off the M25. They followed my car through the woods and down the lane to the Holly Bush at Potters Crouch, where we were made welcome by SS Register members. There was a good turnout of cars, four SSs, three E-types, one XK, two XJs , a Morgan and a Morris Minor. Thanks to fellow Area 57 members for coming along.

BOTANY BAY FESTIVAL OF STEAM - 22nd June

Report by Dick Rogers

We went along to the show in our MK II, taking Martin and our grand-daughter. There was plenty to see with sideshows, stalls and a fairground, plus Classic cars, lorrys and monster trucks. We went into the arena with the Whitewebbs Museum crowd. I even went on the Dodgems (they were fast ones!). A good time was had by all until my granddaughter ran out of money, so then we went home.

REDBRIDGE TOWN SHOW - Valentines Park, 28th-29th June

Report by Graham Smart

Once again superbly organized by Vicky, with a great mix of vehicles. Apart from the cars there was plenty to see and do, including a craft tent, funfair, various stalls, and a soccer skills competion hosted by former West Ham player Tony Cottee.

On Saturday there were 40 cars and several Triumph motorcycles. Club members Brian K., Len and Sally, Keith and Audrey, and Wayne all showed their cars. On Sunday, an even hotter day, there were 55 cars. A splendid turn out considering that the Bromley Pageant and Chigwell shows both clashed. Members attending were Brian K., John and Penny, Brian F. and Linda, and Wayne. Mick and Jaquie were also booked but due to various hoses leaking were unable to make it.

Voting slips were given out for various age groups and categories and Wayne’s superb 1967 Series 1, 2+2 E- type won the car that most people would like to go home in, and came runner up in his class, cars from 1961-1978, well done Wayne.

Car of the day was EDVVS member Tony Eldridge with his magnificent 1934 Austin 7, 2 seater. A really great day out for all the family, lovely weather, and well organized. Well done Vicky and her team of road safety girls. Finally a mention for the marshals, Graham, Wayne and Isle of Wight Mick who got all the cars parked safely, we couldn’t have done it without you!

CLASSIC BAN IN LONDON?

From Motoring press

Now classics are under threat from London authorities who want them banned from the city centre, with underlying implications for the rest of the country. A new study imminently due for publication contains plans that threaten to end the use of classic cars in London with possible implications nationwide. The study examines the possible implementation of so called "Low Emission Zones" (LEZs), that is areas in which vehicles with high emissions, possibly including cars built before 1992, the year when catalytic converters became compulsory on new cars in the UK, would be banned. The full report into LEZs, a joint project by the Association of London Governments (ALG) and the Greater London Authority (GLA), will be available within a matter of weeks. A spokesman for the ALG told CCW that the study is strictly at the "feasibility stage" but added that further steps to reduce pollution in the capital were "essential". Despite the fact that LEZs are not likely to appear in the very near future, if the policy were to be become official legislation, there would clearly be enormous implications for classic car owners in the capital and, having already brought in the unpopular congestion charge in the capital, London's Mayor, Ken Livingstone, has reputedly said that he is considering the adoption of LEZs in all 33 London boroughs. An additional fear is that other cities across the country might be prompted to follow suit reducing the use of classics in built up areas nationwide. At the time of going to press further details are unavailable, but we will bring you the full story as soon as the report is published.
