XJ SPRING WEEKEND

At the end of April, Alan and Barb joined us on the XK Spring weekend to the relatively little known area of Cheshire. Luckily the weather that had been so good for all of April carried on and it was sunshine the whole time. We had a good journey apart from looking for a pub which I eventually found was the right village but wrong road. We also had an unintended drive round Chesterfield when I took a right turn which was wrong instead of a left turn that was right!

The visits arranged for us were to the black and white building called Little Moreton Hall parts of which date back to Tudor times. After a pleasant pub lunch, we drove to the very interesting Anderton Boat lift where boats are lifted 50ft between the River Weaver to the Trent and Mersey canal. This weekend had the most people that have ever been on one of these Sprlng weekends, which meant that organiser Bob Mackman had needed to work out two different countryside routes so that groups of people got to the arranged places at different times. He and wife Sue did a great job of organising it all.

The Saturday night dinner was very good and Chairman Mick Duffy told us that next year would probably be in the Bedfordshire/Buckinghamshire area. Sunday morning was fairly free to go where you liked, and some of us visited a nearby water mill, which rather like the Tardis was very much larger inside than it looked from outside. We then drove to the local Jaguar dealer at Wilmslow where some people took the opportunity to drive the latest XK, before going on to a large nearby restaurant where Sunday lunch had been arranged.

As usual it was good to see lots of the familiar faces that we always meet up with on these weekends but also nice to see lots of newcomers, including Alan & Barb.

I'm glad to see from the following article that Audrey sent that the Museum weekend was much better than last year's.

PORTSMOUTH PARADE April 20 - 22nd '07

Report by Audrey Williams

Sunshine, superb sights, a good mixture of old vehicles, and a scintillating social scene. What more could Dick & Eileen, Eric & Margot, Keith & Bobbie & Keith & myself have asked for? Once again Whitewebbs Museum organised a wonderful weekend.

Keith and I travelled down in advance of the rest of our party, mid morning on Friday, as we were afraid of missing our chance to go up the Spinnaker Tower (Portsmouth's answer to the London Eye but just a static viewing gallery 557 feet high) and take in the great views over Portsmouth Harbour and the Isle of Wight. The programme for the weekend was tightly packed so it was just as well we made the effort. We also had time to stroll down to Emsworth harbour from our hotel, again well worth a visit. The hotel itself was family owned - very pleasant and friendly, with good food this time for all the 75 visitors from the Enfield Museum.

Saturday was taken up with our trip to Portsmouth Naval Dockyard. Our inclusive ticket allowed us to visit the Mary Rose & Museum, HMS Victory, HMS Warrior and a boat trip around the harbour. There were several other attractions, but not enough time.

Saturday evening's entertainment after the meal included the annual 'design a hat' competition, two quizzes and a lively disco. Some of our Jaguar party surprised us all with their hidden talents! Keith W also begrudgingly joined us on the dance floor after muttering several times - "But it's not even New Year's Eve!"

Sunday morning was fully taken with a visit to a Rural Life Centre near Farnham, followed by Sunday Lunch at a nearby restaurant. Unfortunately this was owned by a Swiss family of a particular strict Christian order, so it was NOT licensed. We paid £5 for the privilege of drinking our own wine on their premises - a rather steep corkage charge!!

It was an excellent weekend and incredible value for money. Look out for the yellow ducks at this year's Enfield Pageant. All will be revealed !!
