Newsletter - January 2007
 
I must begin this Newsletter with a couple of apologies; firstly if anyone went to the Legion on New Year's Day and found no-one there, we are sorry.  Several of us were at Dick and Eileen's with the intention of going on to the meeting.  We realised that those of us who were there could well be the only ones going anyway, so we rang a few people to let them know and Dick popped round to leave a note only to find that the Legion wasn't open anyway. The other apology is to Dick Rogers & Keith Oswick as I should have mentioned last month that they are now members of the EDVVS Committee.

Although Christmas & the New Year seem to be fading rapidly into the past, just a couple of recaps of what went on. Surprisingly the Mince Pie night saw a low attendance; some people really don't book their holidays at very good times!!! I gather that quite a few people went to Nazeing on the day after Boxing Day and there was a very good turnout of interesting cars at St Albans on New Year's Day. As this is a gathering organised by the Bentley & Vintage Sports Car Club our cars are mere babes compared to some of the vehicles there. Pride of place probably went to a Stanley Steamer charabanc dated about 1912.

At the last meeting we started to try and get some things organised for the coming year. We have made a provisional booking on April 15th for breakfast at the Woodman at Nuthampstead. Dick & Eric found a pub with good food that could take a group of about eighteen for Sunday lunch. It is The Green Man at Little Braxted near Witham. No dates have been agreed yet but as with some of the other things I'm mentioning, it's a case of watch this space.

In the past , the possibility of Tenpin Bowling has been mentioned, Alan is going to check it out at Bishops Stortford as the one at Stevenage might not be very convenient for people.

Doug and I are going to organise a quiz which would be part Jaguar questions and part general knowledge. Hopefully we would be able to use the television room so that everyone could hear properly.

Several years ago Dick & Eileen organised a games evening, things like Jenga, shovehalfpenny, card games of the cheat variety (nothing serious ) etc. They would be happy to do this again if a suitable date can be arranged.

Hopefully some definite dates can be agreed on by the time I write the February Newsletter. Any other suggestions for outings etc. are always welcome.

Looking further ahead a show at Barleylands near Billericay has been mentioned, but that is not till July so there is plenty of time to find out more about that one. Shows that are more than just a car show are obviously the ones that are of most interest.

On a totally different subject, some of you will remember the great potato contest that Eric & Dick had last year; they intend to do it again but I believe that Eric intends to be the judge this time so Dick is looking for some competion.

A few weeks before Christmas some of the "boys" went to the Ace Cafe on a Sunday morning; they happened to be filming a show in the "Look Ten Years Younger" makeover series for Channel 4, we will probably never hear the last of it, but Eric was one of those interviewed for the programme, due to be shown sometime in March. He assures me that he is not the one getting the makeover.

In March we will be having our Annual General meeting as usual, which raises two points. The John Dyall Trophy is awarded at this meeting for services to the Club; this was originally for services during the previous year, but it was felt that this narrowed the choice too much and it was agreed that it would now be for services throughout the Club's existence. I realise that many of you, while being very welcome members of the club do not take a very active part, so understandably feel that they don't know people well enough to vote. Last year Alan only received eighteen voting slips back, however, as we regularly get twenty plus on Breakfast Runs and we had thirty plus for the Christmas dinner, it seems that some of the more regular members have not been voting either. The previous winners have been Roy, Barbara, myself, Eric, Dick and Keith Oswick and, in my personal opinion, there are two people not on this list who have both done a tremendous amount for the club over the years.

Alan has once again expressed his intention to retire as Area Rep at the AGM, and would like some nominations as a replacement. Obviously any nominee would have to agree to their name going forward.

Press article about the Holly Bush - Meeting place of the XK Register

Guide to British pubs: The Holly Bush Potters Crouch, near St Albans, Herts (01727 851792).

By Christopher Middleton

From the outside, it looks as if a wicked witch has cast a spell on the Holly Bush. The little white cottage is not so much covered in wisteria as strangled by it.

Step inside and the fairy tale continues. The low shiny ceiling, the misshapen wooden beams, the gleaming spick-and-span-ness of the tables, the homeliness of the candelabra and crockery, the tiny red lightshades that look like glowing fairies' corsets — surely this establishment must be the home of house-proud pixies.

Well, of course, it isn’t, this being Hertfordshire and not hobbit country. Even so, you'd be hard put to find a more neatly-put-together landlord than the long-serving Ray Taylor. You know he's called Ray Taylor by the number of framed certificates on the walls testifying to his proficiency in everything from ale preservation to pub-garden maintenance (pheasants pop in from time to time). Even if you didn't know his name, you could match the immaculacy of his suit-and-tie turnout with the symmetry of his certificate deployment. The little laminated notices could well be his work, too: No Smoking At The Bar, No Dogs, No Service At This Bar.

Everything here is calm, orderly, unbuffeted by the winds outside. A menu in symbolically unbending plastic is a roll-call of traditional British bar food circa 1970: smoked salmon sandwiches, prawn sandwiches with Marie Rose sauce, filled baked potatoes and, as a nod to the 1980s, chilli con came (a massive splurge for £6.10)

The bitter, as always, is Fullers, from Chiswick, west London. The ESB (5.5% abv) glides down like a powerful Rolls-Royce. The London Pride (4.1%) is Jaguar-smooth, though with slightly less under the bonnet than the ESB.

Speaking of motorised transport, the Holly Bush stands in open countryside that estate agents would call "twixt the motorways" (Ml and M10), down a pleasant, hedge-free little road called Ragged Hall Lane, no more than 45 minutes on foot from St Albans town centre. It's well worth the walk, too, though it's best perhaps to get a taxi back. Somehow the return journey seems so much further.
