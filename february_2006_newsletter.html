Newsletter - February 2006
 
Quite a number of you will not have heard that Alan had an accident recently. He was knocked off his motorbike and broke his leg. Unfortunately he had a mini stroke while in hospital and is having tests to try and find out the cause. At the time of writing, he was able to get around the ward on crutches so progress is being made. I’m sure the entire club sends its best wishes to Alan for a speedy recovery.

(I have since spoken to Alan on the phone, he is now home and in good spirits. Roy.)

He is adamant that there is no reason to postpone our 20th celebrations on Saturday 25th March. This will be a running buffet throughout the evening from about 7.00 p.m. As I mentioned last time the only cost involved will be your drinks as club funds will be paying for the food. We hope to have all the club photo albums on display and possibly early videos of the club. It will be an informal evening but as you can appreciate, Alan and Barbara need to know numbers so that they can arrange things with the catering staff at Pearse House. Hopefully, as many of you as possible will come along but can you let Barbara know IN WRITING please, by 18th March.

Barbara has started taking names and £5.50 per head for the first Breakfast Run of the season, to Rickling Green on 9th April. Call her for bookings.

It has been mentioned recently that as we have not been to so many JDC events over the past few years, the occasion of our 20th seemed a good time to go as an Area to the JDC 50th celebrations on Sunday 21st May. Dick has had confirmation from Nik Drukker that we can have an area to ourselves at Heythrop Park. This is not very far from Chipping Norton in Oxfordhire; the nearest village is Enstone. We have driven through that area and I know there are plenty of signs to Heythrop Park. Nik needs to know a rough idea of the space we will want, and we will try and collect names within the next few weeks.

As usual Eric will be liaising with the EDVVS for the Enfield Pageant and will be collecting names nearer the time. Our display will be on May 28th and 29th. Bearing in mind our 20th anniversary we hope to make our stand even better than usual. Further into the year, on August Bank Holiday Monday, Dick will be organising passes for the Trent Park Steam Show.

Another couple of dates that are connected with the EDVVS are the car show at Fanshawes, in Hertford on Sunday 2nd July, and the Hoddesdon Town Show on Saturday 8th July. Brian Kingsley will take bookings for the first of these. Most people who have attended Hoddesdon before will hear from them direct.

Some of you will know that Richard Freestone and his Bentley are at present in New Zealand. He has been keeping Keith Williams up to date via E-mail. He seems to be having a good time, starting with a stop in Bangkok to see the sights!! Singapore and Hong Kong were other places visited before reaching New Zealand. The Bentley has been behaving well.

You will shortly receive your voting slips for the John Dyall Trophy, by post. As usual this will be presented at the A.G.M. on 20th March and is awarded for services to the club. Just a reminder that the previous holders have been, Roy, Barbara, myself, Eric and Dick. If you have anything you would like to raise for discussion at the A.G.M., please contact Alan in advance of the meeting.

Finally, thanks to Keith Oswick for organising our visit to the Traffic Control Centre at South Mimms. We were given an interesting talk by Jon Caldwell who is in charge of the Eastern Region. The livery for their vehicles on the motorways is similar to the police but they are part of the Highways Agency. Their main function is to keep traffic moving, whether by means of the variable speed limits, so familiar on the M25, or setting up diversions at accidents. They hope that they now have the number of vehicles and personnel available to prevent the chaos that was on the M11 when people were snowed in for hours. Luckily we have not had the snow to test the system. Everyone seemed to find it an interesting evening and on Alan’s behalf, I have written to Mr Caldwell expressing our thanks.
