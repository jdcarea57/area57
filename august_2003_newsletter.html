VISIT TO SECRET NUCLEAR BUNKER - Sunday 10th August

On what turned out to be the hottest day of the year quite a few of us visited the Secret Nuclear Bunker at Kelvedon Hatch. We had eleven cars lined up in the car park, which we were later walking under apparently. Apart from the interest of the visit most of us were grateful for the 60 deg temperature underground. We left Len and Sally Smerald to roast themselves at Regulo 90 something, hopefully with frequent basting of sunscreen. Down below we were given the audio guides to follow the numbered trail around the site. Alan Collins' commentary didn’t seem relevant to what we were seeing; not surprising as it was from the Palace of Westminster!

A fascinating visit took us 75 feet underground where three storeys housed more than 110 tons of equipment and up to 600 Government personnel. The bunker had its own water supply, electricity generators, BBC studio, radar room and the room where scientists would be able to plot the fall out patterns from any nuclear attack. To really take in all the information provided by photos, maps, videos etc. would take many hours but it was an eye opener to see how serious the powers that be felt the cold war situation was back in the early fifties when this was built, and it was certainly worth a visit.

LONDON TO SOUTHEND CLASSIC CAR CHARITY RUN - Sunday 27th July

Report by Keith Oswick

This event is organised by the South Eastern Vintage and Classic Vehicle Club with the sponsored proceeds raised going to Great Ormond Street Hospital and St. Dunstans organisation for blinded ex-servicemen or women. About £4,800 was raised on the day. Several cars met at the Whitewebbs Museum on an overcast morning for the start of this event and were presented with complimentary t-shirts, baseball caps and balloons and running plaque courtesy of the main sponsors. Excellent route directions were supplied for a lovely scenic country route to the halfway stage at the Basildon Eastgate shopping Centre which was approximately 34 miles. We were greeted by a 3 piece jazz band, complimentary cup of coffee and a heavy shower of rain, but nevertheless spirits were not dampened. Wayne and Vicki Blackburn were there in their Rover P4. The rain stopped, the sun came out and a good selection of vintage and classic cars continued on the 2nd stage to Southend via country lanes , another 34 miles. Excellent reception at Southend upon our arrival with a section of the front promenade from the Casino closed to normal traffic for us to park. About 130 cars paraded along the seafront. Sun, sea and sand welcomed us, the atmosphere was great with quite a few people dressed in period costume, including "John Lennon" and "Rod Steward" lookalikes. A Jazz band and Classic Gold Radio supplied the music which had several people "dancing in the street". There were several awards with the furthest travelled entrant from Dublin and one from Spalding. Car of the show went to a Packard. There were several special awards with a medallion on a ribbon presented to Dick, Eileen and Martin Rogers for their MK2. All prizes and awards were presented by Peter Aimes M.P. for Southend & District. A good day out.

BROOMFIELD PARK PALMERS GREEN CENTENARY CELEBRATION - Sunday 3rd August

Report by Keith Oswick

Penny and John Wilkinson organised a collection of cars and the mobile shop from the EDVVS. John’s MK2 240 and my Daimler 250 were amongst the 15 cars on show. We had a good position in the park under the trees as shelter and shade from the sun, as it turned out to be very hot that day. The show was organised by Enfield Council to promote the proposed restoration of Broomfield House which was severely damaged by fire about 12 years ago, by organising guided tours of the building with information desk and details etc.

Broomfield Park Model Boat Club had a nice collection of model boats on show with lots of detail that took many months to complete. I noticed that only sailing and electric powered boats were using the lake so when I enquired I was informed that internal combustion engine boats were not allowed on the lake and steam powered boats have to have annual boiler inspections and certification like traction engines and railway engines so not many people build them anymore. One tennis court was being used by remote controlled model cars.

Elsewhere there were puppet shows and teddy bears picnic shows for the children plus salsa dancing around the bandstand. There was also an "Its a Knockout" competition going on between 4 teams of people. The funfair did not get going till after 2 p.m. The show was a free entry show so there was a steady stream of people looking at the cars and the mobile shop seemed to be busy. Overall it was a relaxing day out.

"CLASSICS ON THE GREEN" - Wednesday 6th August CROXLEY GREEN (A mini Harpenden)

Report by Keith & Audrey Williams

We had arranged to meet up with our friends (on what turned out to be the hottest evening of the year so far!) at Langleybury Cricket Club. Being local people they were able to lead us down beautiful county lanes to end up on Croxley Green near Watford.

The hosts, Watford & District Classic Vehicle Club, made us very welcome. During the evening some 800 old vehicles assembled on the green including a full range of classics, bikes, commercials, kit cars, American super cars and vintage cars. We thought we had a jet-propelled car there too but it turned out to be a Tatra with an incredible hi-fi system which when started up and shut down exactly mimicked a jet engine!

My favourite exhibit was a collection of silver cross dolls prams dating from 1920. There was an appropriate doll for each pram and dressed in the correct style for that era. Two ladies in period costume pushed the prams up and down the aisles during the course of the evening.

Keith Oswick and Eric Tetsell arrived after work and joined our party for a quick drink! Admission was free but all donations went towards Watford Peace Hospice. A very worthwhile visit - will certainly do it again next year.
