Newsletter - December 2004
 

Area Rep’s Christmas Message

Yet another busy year has passed us by where we had some new venues but still many of the old familiar ones.

The Enfield Pageant was again a great success with a dazzling display of cars, which of course gave us another 1st prize. Thanks to everyone for making this show so popular.

Again the breakfast runs were very well supported, all three of them.

We are looking forward to another Christmas Dinner at the Old manor in Potters Bar, although it may have already taken place by the time you receive your newsletter.

Whilst mentioning the Newsletter, at this time of year I always ask you to put your thinking caps on and write those reports you have always been meaning to do, but didn’t quite get round to doing, so here is another reminder.

Thanks to all of you that have organised different things to do and places to visit over the year, and a big ‘Thank You’ all of you for the support and help over the past year.

Have a very Merry Christmas and a Happy New Year

Alan

Needless to say, there are no recent outings to report, so I’ll just remind you of a few chances to use your cars during the holiday period.

The EDVVS will be having their usual post-Christmas run to The Sun at Nazeing on Monday 27th December.

This time there is a choice of two places to go on New Years Day. As usual the Bentley and Vintage Cars will be meeting at the White Horse at Shenley.

There is also a Classic Transport meeting at the Three Rivers Museum at Rickmansworth. Some of you may not be aware that 10 a.m. on New Year’s Day actually exists, but that is when this gathering starts.

The only trouble with having to put this Newsletter out in time for Christmas is that there is no chance to report on the Christmas Dinner at the Old Manor or the Mince Pie Night, however I’m sure they will both be enjoyable.

(The mince pie night certainly was RF).
