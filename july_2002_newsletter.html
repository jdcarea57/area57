Newsletter - July 2002

As yet, there have been no volunteers to take over from Alan in March. Please keep it in mind and have a word with Alan if you need to know more of what is involved.

In the past few weeks various members have been out and about. Along with Dick and Eileen Rogers, Eric Tetsell and a friend, Doug and I went to XK weekend, which incorporated XK day at Ringwood Hall near Chesterfield, Derbyshire. The weather forecast for the entire weekend had been dubious to say the least, but all we had was a short shower just after arriving at the hotel, where afternoon tea was our immediate priority. The next morning we were given our routes for a tour around the Derbyshire Dales, which I gather was almost last September’s route in reverse. By the time we returned to the hotel, Chris and Mary Engeham had arrived. No particular tables were allocated for the evening’s dinner and it left the two of them at a table on their own, near ours. So after a quick word to the restaurant manager, a bit of furniture moving soon added their table to ours. They won two bottles of wine in the raffle and we won a bottle of champagne. Amongst the various trophies to be awarded was the Chairman’s Trophy and I think Doug was the most surprised person in the room when Brian Davis presented it to him. Surprise changed to embarrassment a few minutes later when it turned out that the hotel manageress had chosen our car as her favourite; so he had to collect another award.

When we first started going to XK days at Burleigh House in the late 80’s/early 90’s, there used to be rows of cars but those days have gone. However, the committee were quite pleased with the number of cars that did come. It is here that the XK is chosen to go forward to the Champion of Champions later in the year. A lot of very thorough polishing was going on all morning by the concours entrants, but we left before the winner was announced.

I understand that several people went to the Matching Green show. Apparently Richard Freestone thought he would pop in for a little while with his Bentley, but had to stay to the end as his car was chosen as Car of the Day.

Despite some very heavy rain, the show at Harpenden attracted the usual hundreds of cars. Eric Tetsell met a couple who always come up from Somerset for a few days to include this gathering. For a virtually unadvertised show, this continues to attract more cars than any other you can think of.

We had eleven cars on display in beautiful sunshine at Hoddesdon. As last year we were arranged around the War Memorial at the end of the High Street. There was a substantial number of cars on display of various ages and, as usual, it was a very relaxing day. Many thanks to Keith and Audrey Williams for their hospitality afterwards.

Alan and Barbara Collins visited the Darling Buds of May show in Kent, but we heard more from Alan about his chase en route with a new Bentley Turbo R than the show.

The new all colour edition of the EDVVS newsletter was very impressive, and Penny Wilkinson, the new editor, gave Area 57 a good write up.

St. Valentines Park, Ilford - Redbridge Show - 30th June 2002

Report by Mick & Jacquie Stubbings

We were very lucky with the weather, it was dry and warm, which allowed us to wander about at our own pace; there was a lot for us to see, this is a bonus.

We were given very good directions and we arrived on time, all exhibitors had to be parked up by 11.00 a.m as this was when the gates would be opened for the public. Once we were parked, first things first, we had a cup of tea. Then it was time to have a look around. The craft tents were packed with people tempting you with their goods, in fact you were spoilt for choice on where to spend your money. Then we strolled around the rest of the grounds. There were the general stalls that are at most car shows, but on top of this they had large areas set aside for talent shows. You could stop and watch for as long as you wanted! Mixed into all this was a fair ground. We went for just the one day, but if you had gone for both days you would still have found more than enough to have kept you fully entertained. A bonus as far as we were concerned, was the fact that the toilets were kept clean, they didn’t smell awful!

I would say that the show was a great success and a big thank you to Vicki and Wayne Blackburn for the work that they put in to ensure a good time was had by all.

The Chiltern Vehicle Preservation Group - Luton Festival of Transport, Stockwood Park - Luton Sunday 9th June 2002

Report by Eric Tetsell

Looking for an event that I had not been to before I decided, after scanning the skies for good weather, that I would support the Chiltern Vehicle Preservation Group at the 11th Luton Festival of Transport. The Chiltern Vehicle Preservation Group is Luton’s answer to Enfield EDVVS. They advertised that a bus would be available to transport any interested party to Vauxhall Heritage Centre, but I didn’t see any buses. Anyway, the format for the show was along the lines of the Enfield Pageant, and why not as it certainly works.

The Ecurie Ecosse car transporter was in attendance with 3 cars all in the Ecurie Ecosse livery, a C type, a XK120, and, if my memory serves me well, a Cooper. All of these cars are campaigned around the racing circuits of Europe and, as a special feature, they gave a demonstration on how it lifts the cars onto the deck. This is achieved by using a hydraulic winch and I must say it was very efficient. Originally a winch from a second world war bomber was used but as the ally casting had deteriorated so badly they decided to err on the side of caution and use a modern device. As I have an original corgi model of the transporter, and the vehicle’s Jaguar connection with Le Mans, it was rather special for me to see the transporter actually working as it was intended.

The show was well supported by auto jumble and auto memorabilia and as I was able to source very rare parts for the window winder of my XK I was a very happy bunny. Unfortunately the skies opened up and curtailed my participation in any other activities. To conclude, the show was well worth a visit and I would have no hesitation in attending next year.
