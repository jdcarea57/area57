STONDON TRANSPORT MUSEUM - 14th November 2004

Thanks to Eric for arranging our trip to this fascinating musum, dedicated to various forms of transport. Obviously, cars form the major part of the exhibits. In the main, they are "ordinary" cars rather than exotics; making it a case of all our yesterdays. Plenty of motor bikes were also there, plus military vehicles, an American fire engine, a couple of gypsy caravans, plus numerous lawn mowers. Outside there is a replica of Captain Cook’s ship "Endeavour" which is still being fitted out internally.

The owner of the Museum, Mr. F.J. Saunders started collecting and renovating vehicles about twenty five years ago and the wish to share these exhibits led to the opening of the museum back in 1994.

In true Area 57 style we had started our outing with Sunday lunch at "The Crown" in the nearby village of Shillington.

A WILTSHIRE WANDER WITH THE EDVVT - 22nd-24th October

Report by Audrey & Keith Williams

The weather forecast was abysmal so once again the E-type had to stay indoors for the weekend! It was a pleasant drive on Friday morning & we stopped off for lunch at a nice hostelry near Farringdon – The Trout at Tadpole Bridge.

Once we had booked in at the Blunsdon House Hotel we ventured into the outskirts of Swindon to a large designer outlet mall. The steam museum was just alongside but there wasn’t enough time to do it justice.

It was a very busy weekend at the hotel – weddings, parties ,other club meets etc. etc. They’re fully booked till February 2005 and they have 168 bedrooms! The meal arrangements were for the carvery so we had to queue with everyone else. The food was enjoyable enough but rather limited choices by the third day!

On Saturday we all went in convoy to the Cotswold Motor Museum at Bourton-on-the-water in the pouring rain. Some interesting exhibits including a 1937 Swallow, vintage caravans & plenty of related paraphernalia. We had an early cream tea afterwards (lunchtime!) and because of the dreadful weather headed back to the hotel to sample the leisure facilities.

On Sunday morning in the sunshine we all drove together again to the Atwell-Wilson Car Museum at Calne. This was a strange little place just behind the owner’s bungalow! The collection began in 1962 and is very varied from a rare Riley 9, to an even rarer Allard Alvis Speed 20, to a Sinclair C5 and some interesting motorcycles. The wedding fleet of 29 cars was impressive – including a model T Ford, the mighty Rolls-Royce & Cadillacs to the diminutive but elegant Little Singer. – all in tip-top condition. We were pleased to see lottery money had paid for the substantial new garages/halls to house the 70+ collection.

We returned to Blunsdon for lunch & presentations. There had been 2 quizes this time over the weekend. One was particularly tricky – the people who got 3/12 answers right won the prize!! We’ll try them out on one of our weekends away . Danny & Mary Smith were presented with a barometer/clock in a smart wooden case for all their hard work in organising the weekend. Sadly it was their last time and George with Sylvie’s help are planning the next one to the New Forest around Easter next year.

Again a very enjoyable weekend and terrific value even though there were only 30+ of us this time. We had one little black sheep (footstool) in our bedroom – but the hotel was being re-furbished throughout – so the flock of sheep at Blunsdon , which some of you may remember – may well disappear forever in the modernisation process !!
