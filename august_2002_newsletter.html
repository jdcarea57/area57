Newsletter - August 2002

As I write this, the forecast for the August Bank Holiday looks promising, so hopefully the Holly Hill Show should be as enjoyable as last year. When I write next month’s Newsletter, I will no doubt be able to give the general opinion of the M.G/Police Show at Chigwell. It seems that most people think this show is not what it was, and last years positioning of the cars did nothing to dispel this view.

A couple of things that there is time to remind you of are the show at St Mary’s School, Bishops Stortford on 22nd September and the Jaguar/Open Day at Whitewebbs Museum a week later. The SS Register has been invited and Nick Drukker is hopeful of getting more of his members along this time. Museum members cars will also be there so hopefully there will be a good turnout.

At the end of July, Richard and Eileen Rogers and ourselves headed off to North Wales again. On our journey there, it seemed as though we were seeing more sports cars than usual; mainly M.Gs and Triumphs but plenty of friendly acknowledgments along the way. The day after we arrived we drove down to Bala for a few supplies. When we returned to the car park we found a Daimler Dart, an M.G.B. and a fifties Ford (can’t remember the model). Looking at their tax discs, they were from the Swansea area. Their owners returned a few minutes later and we all had a friendly chat. They were staying near Tall-y-llyn lake and having a touring weekend. On our homeward journey we kept meeting Riley R.Ms (ten in all) so they must have been having a get together somewhere in the area. Every year, we comment on the good condition of the Welsh roads making it an ideal area for old cars.

Blasts From the Past - Great Saling, Essex

Quite a few of us attended this new event on the calendar on August 11th. First impressions were good as there were plenty of very visible signs along the route and we were met by one of the organisers who asked a youngster on a bike to show us to our plot. That’s when the organisation went pear shaped. Every area he took us to had one car on it, holding it for a particular group. Eventually we found an area that we could claim as our own; not spacious but we had room for our banner. After that, it turned out to be a very enjoyable show.

A two day event, it was the first time this show had been held and it attracted a wide variety of cars of many vintages and makes. Arena events were taking place all afternoon; country dancing, driving skills, tug-o’-war and strongest man, just to mention a few. I think the commentator who announced the various events could have got a job as the speaking clock in his spare time as we kept hearing that something was due to start in exactly three minutes or other very precise timings. Hot and cold refreshments were available and there were a couple of craft tents.

In addition, Carters Steam Fair was on site, a very traditional fair with steam powered Gallopers and, I think I am correct in saying, the only steam yachts still in existence. Needless to say there was a visit to the dodgems by a large Area 57 contingent, which was thoroughly enjoyed by all concerned.

This could become a regular event on our calendar as the general impression was quite favourable.

Harpenden Evening Show Wednesday 10th July

Report by Eric "Scoop" Tetsell

I always look forward to the Harpenden Show so the weather forecast was of particular interest, as it was not good. Anyway, I still attended because in England if you wait for good weather you could wait forever and this show is by far the best evening show I have ever attended.

On arrival at 5.30 p.m. the heavens opened up with a vengeance so John and I decided to open the wine early. This was a big mistake because Keith and Audrey can smell alcohol from Hertford, and their arrival seemed to be more than just a coincidence!

Again, I was astonished at the quality and quantity of cars in attendance, even with the bad weather, although this passed over quite quickly and had by now turned into an excellent evening. The numbers must have been well over a thousand, quite astonishing for an evening show.

Some of the cars I found particularly interesting were a Turner open two-seater sports car (something I had never seen before), a Humber Sceptre diesel, yes diesel.! Two absolutely stunning Triumph Gloria’s mid 1930’s, one belonging to Doug Shipman’s nephew. A Riley Kestrel with the most delicate grill I have ever seen on a sports car, the list is endless. Some cars had even come up from Devon to attend. Most people bring a picnic or a barbeque with them but this year was rather spoilt by the conditions underfoot.

I have been trying to analyse what makes this show so special as it has very little organisation in evidence. Maybe North Hertfordshire has more than its fair share of classics, or maybe they just use them more than the average classic owner. The use of a common is a bonus as it does not incur any charges therefore all the money raised goes to local good causes. This show is not even advertised, and local residents are mainly oblivious to its existence, yet on average it attracts 1,200 vehicles, that’s about one third of Enfield Pageant’s attendance. I think it is quite remarkable. Come along next year and see for yourself.

BLASTS FROM THE PAST SHOW - August 11th (Through the eyes of a ten year old)

Report by Lauren Trussell (Richard Rogers' Granddaughter)

When we arrived at Great Saling we were each given a wristband to wear, so they knew we had paid. After the cars were parked, I had a bacon sarnie with Grandad and the rest of Area 57.

My first stop was the Homemade Fudge and Sweet Stall (Yummy!). I had my hair braided and made a bead bracelet in the Craft Tent. I also made a candle, twice, on the honey stall (Beekeeping).

My Grandad took me to the Steam Fair, Carters I think it was called. We went on the Steam Boat, the scariest ride in the world...it was frightening trying to keep in my seat. I also went on the old fashioned Gallopers and Octopus, all with Grandad. There were lots and lots of old cars to look at, I looked at some.

We took a picnic, but you could have a barbecue hog roast.

I enjoyed my day and the weather was kind.
