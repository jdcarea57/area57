Newsletter - February 2007

Treasurer's Report.

Any Other Business.
Presentation of the John Dyall Trophy.

Can I ask you again to ensure that you use your voting slip (which will be in the post) for your nominations for the John Dyall Trophy, awarded for services to the club. If there are any matters regarding the running of the Club that you wish to raise at the A.G.M. please contact Alan Collins beforehand. Similarly if anyone wishes to stand for any of the positions, again speak to Alan. I would also remind you that Alan has advised us of his intention to stand down as Area Rep. at this A.G.M. and we will therefore be needing someone to take his place.

The date for the first Breakfast Run of the year has been confirmed for April 15th at The Woodman, Nuthampstead, nr.Royston. Barbara is collecting names and £7.50 per head. They have provided very good breakfasts for us here in the past, so why not join us for a pleasant early morning run.

Eric already has passes organised for The Enfield Pageant over the Spring Bank Holiday at the end of May so he will be needing names. Contact him to let him know whether you can show your car/cars on Sunday, Monday or both.

I have been given some dates; some of which are confirmations of provisional dates. There is a show at Battlesbridge in Essex on 13th May; Alan and Barbara have been to this one in the past so can probably tell you a bit more about it. John Clark has been told of a show at Cheshunt on 7th July. (020-8527-2706 for more details). A few years ago Alan and Barbara went to a show held on the farm in Kent where The Darling Buds of May was filmed; it is being held there this year on 15th July.

One of the biggest car events of the year is the Classics on the Common show at Harpenden; a pre -booked event only. I gather that some people have already been E-mailed about this. The date is Wednesday 25th July.

I know it's only February, but I'm going as far ahead as August 19th with a mention of the show held near Burnham on Crouch in aid of Little Haven Children's Hospice. An enjoyable event and a good cause; Dick Rogers will be the contact for this nearer the time.

Alan made some enquiries for us about the possibility of going Tenpin bowling. Unfortunately there was not enough support for the idea and it also seemed that it would not have been possible to make a group booking on a Saturday evening.

Doug and I have managed to put together a quiz to be held on April 16th, which is a normal club night. It is part general knowledge and part Jaguars/motoring. All the questions are multiple-choice, so come along and join in. We have never held a Quiz Night before so hopefully it will make a change and be a bit of fun. There will be a prize for the winner.

I have an order for sweatshirts etc. started but I need at least two more items before I can send it off. If your Club clothing needs replacing let me know. I have always got the catalogue with me at Club meetings.
