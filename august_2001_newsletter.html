Newsletter - August 2001

The summer being well under way, hopefully many of you have been getting out and about in your cars at the various events over the past few weeks. However with one exception, I am still waiting to hear about them. By the time this reaches you, quite a few of our cars will have been to the new August Bank Holiday event at Enfield and the regular show at the Chigwell Police Ground. Although anyone attending National Day at Gaydon might disagree, the weather has been an improvement on last summer. I understand from Chris that attendance at Gaydon was quite low as the appalling weather on the Sunday kept away spur of the moment attendances.

With Dick and Eileen Rogers in their XK150 DHC, we took the E-type to North Wales for a week in July and had a lovely few days of glorious scenery, sunshine and the ideal motoring roads of Wales.

Barbara Collins has started collecting names for the Breakfast Run on 7th October. As usual this is to the Cricketers at Rickling Green, a place visited recently by Michael Winner on BBC1’s Celebrity Sleepover.

Also in October is the visit to the De Haviland Museum which is only a few minutes from J22 of the M25. This is on Sunday 14th at 2 p.m. and nearer the time I would like to know numbers and also how many will be coming back to us for tea and cake.

Don’t forget that I have entry forms for the Cancer Research Show at Stapleford Abbots on 30th September.

CHIGWELL ROTARY CLUB SHOW - 29th June

Report by Jacquie Stubbings

We arrived about 11 a.m. and parked next to Sally and Len Smerald. We could see it was going to be a very hot day, so with sunhats and sun-cream at the ready we set about enjoying the day.

Loraine, Sally and I decided to go around the stalls, leaving the men in charge of the food. We arrived back to find that they had already eaten. We should have known it was a mistake to leave John Clark with anything he could put in his mouth!

Anyway, back to the show; we felt it was better than last year, with more to see and do and more cars, so it is improving.

I would like to add a small thank you to John, who saved the day for me at the recent quiz night, by eating my fish and chips as well as his own; the things he will do for a friend.

JAGUAR CHIEFS KICK UP STINK OVER WORKERS’ DEODORANT

Workers at car giant Jaguar are in a lather over a problem in the paint shop. Bosses discovered that some under-arm deodorants used by the men leave a powdery residue on new cars and could contaminate the gleaming paintwork. The quality assurance team at Jaguar's Halewood factory on Merseyside have been searching for a deodorant which does not contain aluminium oxide, the chemical that produces the residue. Jaguar says it takes product quality very seriously and paint shop workers have already volunteered to have sweat samples taken from their shirts to test the deodorant they use. If it is shown that aluminium oxide is present, the worker will have to shower and put on fresh clothes.

Jaguar bosses have gone a step further and tested more than 25 deodorants to see if they contain the chemical. Only three brands passed the test, one of which was Asda's 99p For Men body spray. The supermarket has agreed to supply Jaguar with 3,000 cans of body spray for the hard-working paint shop technicians. Andy Adcock, Asda’s health and beauty general manager, said: "We're pleased to hear that our men's body spray is as easy on Jaguar's paintwork as it is on the under-arm."
