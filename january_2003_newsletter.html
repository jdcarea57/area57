Newsletter - January 2003

It seems a long while since I wrote the December Newsletter, as it has to be written earlier in the month in order to reach you by post before Christmas. Since then we have had the traditional Mince Pie Night which, most surprisingly, wasn’t as well attended as usual. In fact not all the food got eaten , which must be a first.

On the day after Boxing Day a few of our members travelled to Nazeing to join up with the EDVVS for their usual post-Christmas Run, and on New Year’s Day I understand that there was quite a good turnout of cars at the Bentley meeting at Shenley, in spite of the extremely wet weather.

It’s time to get your diaries out and start entering some dates for the coming year. Our AGM will be on March 17th. As usual, if you have any matters that you would like discussed please speak to Alan Collins beforehand. Likewise, if anyone wants to take over any of the regular jobs, again speak to Alan. He will be unable to attend any meetings before the AGM, so please ring him if you wish to raise any points about the club and how it is run.

Barbara has booked the Breakfast Runs to the Cricketers at Rickling Green for Sunday April 27th and Sunday October 5th. In Barbara’s absence I have started taking names and payments for these.

On Sunday May 4th there is a Rotary Club show at Hartham Common near Hertford. It is officially for vehicles over 25 years old, but Alan intends to find out if we can put on a club display in which more recent vehicles can be included.

On the following day, Bank Holiday Monday, it is track day at Goodwood; Alan has details of this, but I believe they are also usually in the Jaguar Driver.

Other dates I have been given are; the Rotary Club Show at Chigwell on 29th June, which Mick & Jaquie always recommend, and The Darling Buds of May show in Kent is on July 13th.

Looking further ahead to September, Dick Rogers has been sent details of a show on the 14th at the Institute of the Motor Industry near Hertford. This is in aid of the Ataxia Rehabilitation Unit at Rayleigh, which helps neurological childhood diseases. The show will be held in the grounds of the I.M.I., with space for about 100 vehicles. Although food and drink will be available, there will not be any other entertainment. All vehicles displayed will receive a plaque, and a prize will be awarded for the best kept vehicle.

An observation from Mick Stubbings

The Jaguar E-type is now over forty years old, that’s since it was first produced but, according to Auto Car magazine it has recently been voted Number 1 in a poll to find the world’s MOST BEAUTIFUL CAR.

It came top in a list of one hundred cars which included Ferrari, Porsche, Lamborghini.

For a car designed in the late fifties and early sixties to still be that desirable in 2003 says a lot for the design team!
