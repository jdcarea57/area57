Newsletter - June 2005
 

If there is anyone who hasn’t heard, we were placed second at the Enfield Pageant Of Motoring. This year the excellent M.G. Owners Club stand pipped us. On both days we had a really good display which Eric had organised again for us. As usual, the bacon rolls were appreciated. The Pageant is the event that really calls for a lot of team work with organisation, transportation etc. and at the next meeting Alan thanked everyone who had been involved. In addition, Alan received a letter from the EDVVT thanking us for our assistance with marshalling at the show.

Brian and Linda surprised most of us by bringing along their wedding photos from a few days earlier. Congratulations to the new Mr and Mrs Ferrett from us all.

Needless to say, our club calendar as well as the one in the Jaguar Driver have plenty of events for you to go to. Don’t forget that a write up, however short, is appreciated.

It seems that 18th September is a popular date. The Croxley Green show is already in the calendar and it now appears that the St. Mary’s School classic car show in Bishops Stortford and the show at Fanshaws are also on that date. Brian Kingsley has entry forms for the latter which is at The Institute of the Motor Industry at Brickenden.

By the way, Keith and Audrey have asked me to pass on their usual invitation for a cuppa after the Hoddesdon show on July 9th.

ROCK N' ROLL DAY - WALTHAM CROSS Saturday 11th June

Report by Keith Williams

On what should have been a blazing June day (actually it wasn't too bad and stayed dry but with a cold wind), Tom, Brian, Dick & Eileen & Martin, and myself attended a classic car event in Waltham Cross on Saturday 11th. There were 24 cars booked in but unfortunately only 12 turned up (of which 4 were from our club!). The remainder, though, formed a good mixture including a couple of American gas-guzzlers, an Austin-Healey 3000 and a beautiful 1930s Allard.

The highlight of the day was a live band (The Morebeats Blues Band) who got the town rocking with their rendition of numbers from the Beatles, Stones , Eric Clapton and Fleetwood Mac to name but a few. They were later accompanied by a dance troop called Just Jivin' who encouraged the passers by to have a dance or two! (Incidentally they hold their dance club night in the British Legion on Holtwhites Hill on a Thursday - for those who might wish to freshen up on their jiving technique!

In contrast, the most exciting part of the day was Dick administering first aid to a lady who decided to have an epileptic fit right in front of his car.

All in all an enjoyable day but not a lot to do in Waltham X and no prizes or judging of cars.
