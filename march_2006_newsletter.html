Newsletter - March 2006
 
Monday 21st March - Minutes of the 20th A.G.M.
 
Fifteen members were present at the A.G.M., lower than usual but apologies for absence were received from several people. Area Rep. Alan Collins thanked us for coming and it was agreed that the minutes of last year’s meeting should be taken as read.

Area Rep’s Report

As usual, Alan gave us a brief run through of events, social gatherings etc. that had taken place over the past year. The Enfield Pageant is always the main event and he thanked Eric for his organisation, Clockhouse Nursery via Keith Oswick for floral decorations, and the cooking squad for the always enjoyable bacon rolls. The Hoddesdon Show and Trent Park are also EDVVS events that we supported. Despite dreadful weather, the Harpenden show was still well supported. Apparently the show at Waltham Cross was only small and the Enfield Town Show and Chigwell didn’t get very enthusiatic reports. We fitted in three Breakfast Runs this year, the two usual ones at Rickling Green, plus an extra at the Woodman at Nuthampstead. We hope to do the same this summer. The EDVVS had a weekend in the New Forest which was enjoyed by Keith & Audrey and Dick & Eileen. The XK Register weekends were in the Witney area, Stamford/Peterborougn and Portsmouth. The Goodwood Revival meeting got glowing reports from Richard Freestone and Dick has continued to visit the Ace Cafe regularly. One of our most recent outings was the interesting trip to the Highways Agency at South Mimms that Keith Oswick organised for us. Keith Williams and Brian Kingsley both celebrated their 60th birthdays and several of us helped Tom celebrate his 80th. Eileen’s cake for Brian was appreciated. The Christmas dinner at Pearse House was enjoyed by everyone. Obviously I may have missed out some places, so apologies if I’ve left out anything that you attended.

Alan closed by thanking everyone in the Area for their support and friendship.

Treasurer’s Report

Barbara Collins circulated our Accounts Statement which looks very healthy, for this reason the Club is able to pay for the food at our 20th celebrations.

Secretary/Newsletter Editor’s Report

Wendy Shipman thanked Roy and Kim Fludgate for the production and circulation of the Newsletter and as usual asked that people do read it and contribute to it.

Membership Secretary’s Report

Roy Fludgate reported that we have had two lapsed memberships, however through the web site we have gained two new members. There are currently 41 memberships representing 61 people and 69 vehicles. There have been plenty of hits on the web site and the Club Calendar is updated more regularly than the Newsletter of course.

Election of Officers

The election of officers caused more discussion than usual as Alan felt that some people were of the opinion that he might wish to take a break after fifteen years. As has happened before there was no-one else who wished to take on the post. Alan has put in a lot of commitment over the last fifteen years which we all appreciate. However, the way in which we have organised the club over it’s entire twenty years means that it has never been a one man show or even one in which decisions are made by the committee. All members have always been free to suggest anything about the club’s organisation, outings etc. so nothing would change if Alan were to stand down. It was then unanimously agreed that Alan would continue as Area Rep., supported by Barbara Collins, Wendy Shipman and Roy Fludgate. As Phil and Heather Kendon hope to move, Brian Kingsley has agreed to take over the positon of Club Archivist. Please remember to take photos for the Club albums, with the date and place on them.

Other Business

There was only one matter raised under ‘Any Other Business’. In the past voting for the John Dyall Trophy has been for services to the Area over the past year. It has been agreed that in future this will be awarded for general help to the Area regardless of a current year. Alan also mentioned that the people who bother to vote has declined, so maybe this may make it easier.

It only remained for Alan to thank everyone again for twenty good years.

Presentation of The John Dyall Trophy

Valerie Dyall was with us again to present the John Dyall Trophy. A clear winner this year was Keith Oswick. Well done Keith.

With the A.G.M. concluded we then began the usual meeting.

Alan is continuing to hobble around on crutches and thanks everyone who sent him get well cards or visited him in hospital.

As has been mentioned in past Newsletters, it has been decided that our 20th anniversary would be an approriate time to support the JDC’s 50th. If you read the March issue of the Jaguar Driver, you will have seen that the event has moved. It will now be held at Eynsham Hall, North Leigh near Witney. It is on the A4095 between Bicester and Witney. Dick has been in contact with Nid Drukker who says we can have an area for parking together. Please let Alan or Dick know by the next meeting (3rd April) if you want to go so that Nik knows how much room to reserve for us.

Mick Duffy, chairman of the XK Register, has contacted Alan to say that our support would be appreciated at XK Day on 30th July at Burghley House, Stamford. This is the final day of International XK weekend, during which we always enjoy a country drive on the Saturday. Last year other car clubs were invited and all cars park together, not XKs and others. Burghley House is a very impressive stately home and the Orangery Restaurant provides good food, both hot and cold. The Register gets excellent support from local Jaguar dealer, Marshalls of Peterborough who will bring along new cars to try.

Alan has details of a parade of cars in the Westminster area on 16th July, contact him if interested.

Dick has said that anyone is welcome to join him at the SS meeting at Potters Crouch on 10th July.

The Chilterns Area have a change of venue for their Area day this year. It is at the Buckingham Railway Centre on 2nd July. Shuttleworth is the setting for XJ Day again on 11th June which this year is a flying day.

Brian K. has passed on some leaflets from the Chelmer Valley Car Club which may be of interest to those of you in the Essex direction. They are holding a Classic Car Show in conjunction with the Weald Park County Festival on 17th-18th June, and Classics On the Common at the Horse and Groom, Galley Wood Common on Wednesday 12th July. I have an entry form for the first of these and 01245-268157 is the number to ring for Classics on the Common.

The Harpenden Classics on the Common meeting had been in some doubt but I gather it is going ahead although I have not been given a date.

I have just booked a weekend away at Broom Hall Hotel near Thetford for 8th and 9th September. The hotel only has fifteen rooms and we already have over half of these so please contact me if you want me to see if there are any more rooms available.

I apologise for putting the A.G.M. date wrong in the January Newsletter but it was correct in the Calendar and the February Newsletter.
