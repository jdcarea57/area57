Newsletter - September 2005
 

The last few weeks seem to have been fairly active, starting with the show near Burnham that Dick has reported on for me. As agreed, the £40 raised at the Pageant from the sale of the floral displays was given to the Little Haven Children’s Hospice.

The weather for the Trent Park Show was considerably better than last years and we had a pleasant relaxing day in the sunshine after the obligatory bacon rolls. Quite a few of us did several parades around the arena accompanied by Dave Norton’s usual knowledgeable commentary. Barbara and Loraine managed to spend a considerable time satisfying their shopaholic needs.

The Buntingford show on September 3rd was again sunny; not as hot as last year but there was a sunny side of the street for Len and Sally. Their car was chosen as second in the category for Best Condition Car. There was a wide variety of cars although generally not too many oldies. In the back of a "Funny" car which I think started life as a Ford Popular was a sign saying "Get In, Sit Down, Hold On and Shut Up". The motor bike fraternity was very well represented. Two live bands were playing and I understand that one heavy rock band attracted a very young fan who not only played "air guitar" but "air saxophone" as well.

The next day we tried a new venue for a Breakfast Run. Apologies to anyone who may not have heard about this in time. We had a very enjoyable breakfast at The Woodman, Nuthampstead, near Royston. It was a glorious morning for a drive and everyone seemed very impressed with the breakfast, so I would think there will be another visit there at some time. Some people had a busy day and went on to either the Goat or the Capel Manor Show afterwards.

Our return visit to the Corse Lawn Hotel near Tewksbury was very successful. Unfortunately, I didn’t book the good weather as well, but I gather we were better off there than at home. While enjoying some lovely sunshine on the Friday afternoon, Keith and Audrey had a phone call from Laura to say their dog was scared of the thunderstorm. That was the last sunshine we had however and our visit to Worcester on Saturday had showers and cloud. Luckily only cloud while we had a boat trip along the River Severn. There were sixteen of us this year, more than we have had for a long time, if not ever, so we tended to split up into smaller groups after that. Some of us snapped up some bargains in the Royal Worcester Seconds Shop. Our sympathies went to Keith and Bobbie who had a call informing them that that they had been burgled. On Sunday morning most of us went to Hidcote Manor Garden, driving through some pretty Cotswold villages on the way.

This was the first time that Alan and Barbara had been able to join us since way back, and they were as impressed with the hotel as the rest of us were last year. It was Eric and Margot’s first visit as they were away last year and it got the thumbs up from them as well.

I think Brian Kingsley was our only representative at the Enfield Town Show and Croxley Green this year, and Dick had a good evening at the recent Classic Car night at the Ace Cafe.

Richard Freestone was extremely enthusiastic about the Goodwood Revival Meeting which has been running for about five or six years now. The revival theme extends to the spectators, with period costumes much in evidence. Hopefully some of you saw the one hour programme about the weekend on Sunday 25th on ITV.

Margot has recently given me an article about actress Daryl Hannah’s trip to Belize to see the work of the World Land Trust. The trip was made possible by Jaguar Cars; the company has supported the Programme for Belize for 25 years, funding conservation projects and helping to set up the Cockscomb Basin Jaguar Reserve.

FUND RAISING RALLY in Aid of The Little Haven Children’s Hospice - Sunday 21st August

Report by Dick Rogers

The show was at Althorne in Essex near Maldon. We arrived in sunshine and it stayed that way all day long. Ron and Pam, Brian, Keith, Bobby and Keith’s mum, Keith and Audrey, Alan and Barbara, Eric and Margot, Eileen, Martin and myself all enjoyed a pleasant day. Things to do or see ranged from Belly Dancers, Singers, Horses, Dogs, Motor bikes, Cars, Tractors, Stalls and a garden to look around, plus a garage full of memorabilia. I would like to thank everyone for going and let you know that the show raised about £5000, more than double last year’s amount.

CHIGWELL SHOW - Sunday 4th September 2005
 
Report by Mick & Jacquie Stubbings
 
As we promised we went to the Chigwell Show but only as visitors, we did not display the car.
 
Fors:-
The car display was back down at the bottom of the show ground.
The arena did have some displays; birds of prey, police dogs and children’s entertainment.
The police helicopter was open to the public and after the dog display you could go and talk to the police and fuss the dogs. They had the smallest police dog in service, she sniffs out explosives and they had a dog sniffing out drugs.
 
Against:-
Most of the cars on display were Mgs.
The fairground stalls catered mainly for children, there were no dodgems.
There were not many stalls to browse around, no craft tents and it used to be nice to see new car dealers displaying.
The tent where we used to all go to get our bacon rolls and tea/coffee was not there and places to get refreshments were sparse.
 
All in all the show has improved. They seem to be trying, but if you were displaying for the whole day there is still not enough to entertain you.
