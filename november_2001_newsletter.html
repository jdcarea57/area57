XK WEEKEND - CHESTERFIELD - 28th-30th September

Report by Eileen Rogers

It was decided by the XK Register to resume the annual XK weekend with a presentation dinner on Saturday night. Unfortunately Doug and Wendy Shipman with their XK120 were unable to go at the very last minute (morning of departure). Doug had an extremely bad cold and was very sadly missed, so only two XKs set off on Friday morning, making their way to Chesterfield in Derbyshire. The occupants of the 2 XKs were Eric Tetsell with friend John, and Dick and Eileen Rogers. The usual refreshment stops were made along the way; a very nice inn called The Blue Cow in Lincolnshire can be recommended.

We arrived at the hotel in time for afternoon tea. The Ringwood Hall Hotel is under new management and is undergoing restoration, but we were made to feel extremely welcome by the friendly staff. There were about 20 Xks for the weekend, with their owners of course!

Saturday morning we set off on the rally with our hood down despite a bit of drizzle. This proved to be a mistake however. Several miles down the road, in the midst of the beautiful Derbyshire countryside, torrential rain descended upon us. By the time we had our hood up we were soaked to the skin.

The village we stopped at for lunch had an old-fashioned cheese shop and nearly everyone made purchases there. As we went back to our cars parked at the side of the road an excited man asked us to wait as his friend who lives close by was driving into the village to look at the cars. She had one of her own in Switzerland. We obliged and stayed chatting for ages.

The XK presentation dinner took place back at the hotel, and Eric Clowe was presented with the chairman’s trophy, Rose Eaton won the Stuart Lightfoot Trophy and, lo and behold, Eric Tetsell won the free weekend for two, and believe it or not, he was speechless.

Sunday, XK Day was WET.

HOW PHOTOS CAN DECEIVE - A Day Trip to the Isle of Wight

By Dick Rogers

On 18th October I was asked by a friend to look at a Mk V Jaguar Saloon 1950 with a 2.5 litre engine.

We had already looked at 2 albums of restoration photos and we were told that only the woodwork was left to re-veneer in order to complete the car.

At the crack of dawn (6.30 a.m. to be precise), we set off in the rain down to meet the hovercraft, which only takes 10 minutes to reach the Isle of Wight. We met the two brothers who owned the car and travelled by Ford Probe at a rapid pace through to Shanklin. The barn was opened and that is when things started to go wrong;

The restoration had been an on-going project since 1994, so we expected to see a nice car, however the car had not been started for about 4 years, so the garden mower battery that they connected up was not going to start it. The chrome work we were told had been re-chromed but it was all rusty and peeling off. The tyres were van type with deep treads, a bit like Town and Country. The wings had been off but were all red rust on the underside. The bonnet did not fit. The head lining was made from an old table cloth. The doors were rusty at the bottom. The seats were the best part of the car as they had been re-leathered as were the door panels. We decided to leave the car, and got a lift back to the hovercraft. Before coming home we had fish and chips and an ice cream. (Thanks Keith for a good day out).

He was disappointed with the car, but has subsequently bought a nice Daimler 250.
