Newsletter - March 2002

Barbara’s list for the Breakfast Run on 21st April is filling up rapidly. She needs names and money (£5.50 per head in advance) if you wish to join this always enjoyable start to the season.

The Hoddesdon Town Centre Show will be taking place on Saturday 13th July.

I have been given details of a show held by the Chiltern Vehicle Preservation Group to be held at Stockwood Park, Luton on 9th June. They have classes for the same sort of variety of vehicles as at the Pageant. Entry is free for pre-booked vehicles but there is an entry charge on the day. The event has been running for eleven years and entry to the event also includes a collection of horse drawn vehicles and the Transport -Section of the Stockwood Park Museum. It is also the only day that the Vauxhall Heritage Centre is open to the public.

Don’t forget that I am now able to get T-shirts with the embroidered Area 57 Club logo.

CLASSIC CAR SHOW - Alexandra Palace, 9th-10th March

Report by Dick Rogers

Having decided to attend on the Saturday, I then drew the short straw and took Eric with me (just joking Eric). We had to queue for about twenty minutes for tickets, the advertised price of which was £8, on the day, however, the price had risen to £9.

Sterling Moss opened the show, and in the main hall there were about forty classics for sale from dealers, taking up the space that normally houses trade stands and autojumble. There were also about eighteen private classics for sale. Stanley Mann, the Bentley dealer had put on a good display, however overall we were somewhat disappointed with the show and, after about three hours, decided we had seen everything there was to see, had some lunch, and departed.

A Weekend in February

By Heather Kendon

February 1st came in with gales and torrential rain, as many will remember from the news reports on television. There were many overturned lorries and numerous accidents. However, despite the weather we travelled down to Bristol to spend the weekend with our daughter Karen. We arrived safely at her house, only to wait outside for her return from work, Karen having also been delayed in traffic by the awful weather conditions. Some hours later Karen’s boyfriend Darren, arrived from London down the M4, and his words were "I have aquaplaned home".

Our main reason for the visit was to accompany both of them to the Royal Bath and West Showground for the Bristol Classic Car Show. Karen had joined the local Morris Minor Club in Bristol to get to know the members and to have some help and advice to purchase a classic Morris Minor. With the help of the Chairman, Karen was able to purchase her dream car, a 1969 Morris 1000 2 door saloon, in snowberry white, with red interior.

On Saturday which was a very wet and miserable day, the four of us set off in her Morris Minor for the show. Karen was to spend an hour or so helping to man her Club stand. The whole of the show was inside several large buildings, with tented walkways from one to the other, thankfully protecting us from the elements.

The first building housed an extensive auto-jumble of all sorts. We parted company, Karen and Darren to find her Club stand in the second building, and Phil and myself to browse around the auto-jumble. Having looked around for some time Phil found some car horns for the Mark 7, and asked to hear their sound. A very loud sound echoed in this large building, and unbeknown to us Karen and Darren were just re-entering the building to find us. On hearing these noisy blasts Karen remarked "I bet that’s my Dad", and could not believe it when they found us purchasing the horns.

We continued our tour together to the Club stands; lots had theme’s for which they were judged. A great deal of thought had gone into each one, and being inside they could be properly stage managed. Karen’s club stand had the members dressed in White Shifts with wings on their backs depicting angels. Their stand had a white fence surrounding billowing white clouds of crepe paper, with heavenly music playing in the background. In the centre an illuminated roadway, with a motorway sign "Scrapyard Heaven", led up a slope to an archway with pearly gates under which sat a Bulldog adorned with a halo. Amongst the clouds were parts of Morris Minor’s in a very sad state, including half a rusted Morris, the back end of another, all depicting a tableau of Heaven for Morris Minor’s. The whole effect was extremely well done, and attracted a lot of interest. They were judged second. The Volvo stand with members dressed as Vikings came first. Having had a good day and with tired feet we returned back to Bristol, the weather having improved just a little. Karen’s little car managed all the hills, although a little slower than our Jag might have done.

Karen is attending maintenance classes so that she can carry out most of her own repairs and maintenance. This has however resulted in a few calls recently to Dad for a bit of help. Karen is looking forward to many happy miles in her little car if and when we have some summer.

We departed Bristol on the Sunday after having lunch in a nice country Pub in that elusive sunshine. However, on reaching Swindon we were back to torrential rain for the rest of our journey home.

It was a good weekend, different and interesting, only we were disappointed that there was an empty stand where the Jaguars should have been.
