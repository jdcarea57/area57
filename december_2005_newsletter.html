Newsletter - December 2005
 
 

Area Rep’s Christmas Message

Well here we are again at the end of yet another year. A year that has seen 3 major hospital operations, 3 major birthdays, 3 breakfast runs, the list goes on and on.

Next year sees our 20th Anniversary as Area 57 and many ideas and suggestions have been made about the where’s and the when’s, so hopefully we will come up with something to suit all tastes.

The Christmas Dinner at Pearse House went off extremely well, with many good reports about the plentiful home-cooked food, and the lovely ambience about the place. The huge log fire in the entrance hall was very welcoming, and the team looked after us very well.

Once again we’ve achieved so much over the past year as a club and its success is down to the efforts of all of you. If we haven’t already had the Mince Pie night by the time you read this, which is on Monday 19th, I hope to see you all there or as many of you that can make it, as we always have a good turn out and normally far too much food.

For those of you unable to attend the Christmas Dinner your piece of Brian Kingsley’s birthday cake will be waiting for you there.

I would just like to take this opportunity to wish everyone a very Merry Christmas and a Happy & Healthy New Year, and hope that 2006 is a good year for everyone.

Alan

One advantage of the Christmas Dinner being early in the month, is that I have a chance to write something about it, before mid-January, when it is fading into the past.

This was our first visit to Pearse House at Bishops Stortford, and it seems to have been enjoyed by everybody. Suggestions that we return there next year have already been received. When Doug and I arrived, we were met by the young ladies offering orange juice or Buck’s Fizz, a lovely open fire, plus a fairy, a Christmas pudding, a sultan, and a jaguar (feline variety), coming down the grand staircase. Eileen and Dick were the fairy and the Christmas pud, and Margot, the cat. Eric was the sultan, or maybe one of the three wise men (or perhaps not!!) Keith O. and Heather also opted for fancy dress as a chef and a middle eastern lady.

It had been decided that we would use this occasion to celebrate Brian Kingsley’s 60th birthday, even though mince pie night is nearer. Eileen had made a cake, complete with an E-type on the top. The saga of Alan’s white sports shirt continues, and it is now in Brian’s possession after we passed it on to him, wrapped and disguised in a box.

As usual, the flying baloons were popular, and Margot provided our table with crackers containing numbered whistles. We just about managed a recognisable rendition of Happy Birthday.

Thanks to Alan and Barbara for organising a very enjoyable evening.

As usual, there are a couple of dates during the holiday period for anyone wishing to blow the cobwebs away. The EDVVS are having their regular run to The Sun at Nazeing on December 27th. The meeting for Bentleys and other interesting old cars on New Year’s Day has a change of venue. This time it will be at the Pre Hotel, Redbourn Road, St. Albans.
