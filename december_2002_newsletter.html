Newsletter - December 2002
 

Area Rep’s Christmas Message

Yet another year has whizzed past full of events. Some were new venues for us but there were still quite a few familiar ones, and along the way we made some new friends.

Once again the Enfield Pageant was the highlight of our year, with the usual hard work being put in by many of our area members, and my thanks go out to so many of you for your support.

We had two excellent breakfast runs, and by the numbers that attend these two events at the times of year when the weather can be so unkind shows just how much they are enjoyed.

The Christmas Dinner, again well supported, and for the second time in our history actually held in Enfield, took place in the Conservatory of the Plough in Crews Hill, with a real Christmas atmosphere. Unfortunately there were a few absent friends, which meant a distinct lack of gadgets and gimmicks.

These events don’t just happen, it takes a lot of hard work to organise and make them successful, so my thanks go to everyone who gives a helping hand.

Spare a thought also for our newsletter editor, and while you’re sitting at home on these long dark dreary nights, why not put pen to paper and write a report on an event that you went to in the summer so that Wendy doesn’t have to think of every item of news.

Our thoughts at this time reach out to both Eric and Margot, and Tom and Enid in what are undoubtedly difficult times for them and we hope that the New Year sees better things in store.

And finally, my personal thanks to all of you for your continued help and support over the past year.

I WISH YOU ALL A VERY MERRY CHRISTMAS AND A SAFE JOURNEY INTO THE NEW YEAR.

Alan

As last year, our Christmas Dinner was at The Plough, and thirty seven of us were there to enjoy a pleasant evening. I’ve had favourable comments on the evening and if the noise level is any indication of enjoyment, it was a success. Last year’s had seemed a little subdued, compared to some of our Christmas meals but this seemed to go well. Thanks to Barbara for organising it, as usual.

If anyone needs to blow away some cobwebs, hangovers etc. on New Year’s Day, the Bentley and Vintage Car Clubs will be having their traditional meet at Shenley. The pub is different this year; they now meet at The White Horse, 37 London Road, Shenley. There is usually a good collection of cars with our Jaguars often being the youngsters.

It has been suggested that we make up a party to go Ten Pin Bowling in the New Year. The nearest centre to the Legion is the David Lloyd Centre in Carterhatch Lane. They apparently have plans to scrap their lanes in order to build tennis courts, so if we want to do this, we’ll have to make a booking for sometime in January. Maybe we can get some names together on 6th January meeting and arrange it.
