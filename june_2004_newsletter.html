Newsletter - June 2004

By now, I expect that the majority of you will be aware that we had another successful Enfield Pageant. We were awarded first prize in the competition for Best Club Stand, which puts £250 in our kitty. Although Doug and I were not able to be there on the Sunday, I gather that the display got a lot of attention.

Thanks go to Brian Stevens for bringing along his XJ220 and Lister XJS and Steve Whitford who brought along a brand new S-type diesel complete with TV in the back. Everyone who brings their cars on either or both days helps to create the excellent display that we put on every year.

As usual Eric organised everything for us as far as passes and booking is concerned. Don’t forget he will be needing names for the Town & Country Show at Trent Park on August Bank Holiday Monday.

A big thank you also goes to the Clockhouse Nursery, Forty Hill for once again supplying us with planters for our stand, and to Keith Oswick for arranging this. These were sold at the end of the event to raise funds for Martin Rogers’ Day Centre.

Since the Club started displaying on two days, this was the first time that we had not been there for both. Why do I get the impression that my cakes were what was missed most on the Sunday!!

At a recent Club meeting, we were chatting about why we don’t seem to do so much together as a club as we did in earlier years. I think a lot of the reason is that many events are repetitious, so to use the well know phrase, "we’ve been there, done that, and got the T-shirt". As a result we have tried to sort out a few new places and organise a couple of social occasions.

6th JUNE - Museum of Power, Langford near Maldon

Report by Dick Rogers

The trip to the Show turned out to be a test of endurance. Six cars were booked, our Mk2, Alan and Barbara’s E-type, Phil and Heather’s M7 made it; three out of six. Bill Starling’s E-type had a petrol problem, Eric suffered a broken oil pipe on the M25 and had to be towed home. Keith and Audrey’s car was okay but Keith’s knee wasn’t! (hope it gets better soon). Brian Kingsley was bullied into going by Ron Risk as his car needed a run, so he turned up. A good day was had by all with plenty of Eileen’s food. The museum is like a larger version of Whitewebbs with seven acres of grounds. It was nice to see Club members prepared to travel further over to Phil and Heather’s and Alan and Barbara’s way for a change.

Thanks to all who attended.
