Newsletter - August 2006
 

I hope that everyone who intended to go to the BBQ at the M.G.Club managed to hear of the change of date from the 21st to the 14th August. It turned out to be a rather disappointing evening. We turned up in force with twelve cars and sixteen people. Although they put on a very good BBQ it was definitely a case of them and us, partly I think because not many of their members were there (despite the change of date being at their request). There really seemed to be three separate groups as we believe some of those present were from the Botany Bay Cricket Club. Although they have a lovely area, the car park is some way from the club house so there was no mingling to look at other people’s cars.

This year was the first time that Doug and I have managed to get to the Harpenden Show and as other people have always said, it does attract about 2,000 cars of all shapes, sizes and ages, as well as motor bikes. A hot day finished with a thunderstorm and we left in heavy rain which had stopped by the time we had gone half a mile down the road.

We had a very hot weekend at Peterborough and Stamford for XK weekend. A very pleasant eighty mile drive around the countryside was organised for Saturday; everyone agreed it was one of the best runs we have had. At the Saturday dinner, chairman Mick Duffy presented the various annual awards followed by a light hearted quiz. On Sunday morning we rejoined the A1 from the Peterborough Marriot, where we had been staying, for the journey to Stamford and XK Day at Burghley House. With perfect timing, Alan and Barbara came along at the same time, as they were joining us for the day, along with Tony and Lesley.

Ten of our cars were on display at the Show in aid of the Little Haven Hospice held at Althorne near Burnham on Crouch. It was a very pleasant relaxing day with plenty to look at; cars, tractors, stationary engines, owls, as well as craft stalls. The £40 raised for selling the planted pots that Keith O’s son provided for the Pageant is given to this charity. The day finished with tea and cakes at Dick and Eileen’s caravan at Burnham.

Several of us are going to the Car Show at Croxley Green, near Rickmansworth on Sunday 17th September. I gather it attracts a large number of cars; spaces can’t be booked but we will try and keep an area when we get there at about 9.30 a.m. An added attraction is the Carter’s Steam Fair; going on previous experience, their dodgems are excellent. It is a long while since Area 57 have had a chance to enjoy some dodgems.

Barbara has booked the last Breakfast Run of the season and by general agreement, we are returning to the Woodman at Nuthampstead on October 8th. She has started collecting names for this and no doubt will be starting another list for the Christmas Dinner on 2nd December at Pearse House.

Finally, very belated congratulations to Ron and Pam Risk who tied the knot back in June. Very few people knew beforehand and I own up to forgetting to put it in last month’s newsletter.
