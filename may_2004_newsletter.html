Newsletter - May 2004

On Sunday 25th April we had our first Breakfast Run to the Cricketers at Rickling Green since the new owner took over. Everything went smoothly, although some felt that the bacon could have been better. For only the second time since the Breakfast Runs started, the two groups, starting from Enfield and Chingford met up at a roundabout just outside Bishops Stortford, and all arrived at the pub in convoy. It was a nice morning so we were able to enjoy tea, coffee etc. in Alan and Barbara’s garden afterwards.

XK SPRING WEEKEND - 7th to 10th May

The area this year was the Sussex Weald, based at a hotel just outside Hailsham. It was the greyest weekend we have had, but as the rain was all overnight we couldn’t complain. As usual the majority of people were regulars but it is always good to see a few new faces and two of the first time couples fitted in very well.

On Saturday morning we had a short drive to the Bentley Wildfowl Trust which also has a small motor museum. Most of the vehicles are privately owned and there is a great range there. Apart from children’s pedal cars they ranged in size from a Sinclair C5 to huge American cars. The two curators were very enthusiastic and were more than happy to let people through the ropes to get a good look. They lifted bonnets, started engines and couldn’t have been more helpful. We had a very pleasant wander around the lakes and pools that are home to hundreds of ducks, geese and swans, plus flamingos and cranes. Unfortunately the future of the Wild Fowl Centre and the Museum is uncertain at the moment as it seems as though the County Council wants to sell it.

Our arranged afternoon visit was to the Hidden Springs Vineyard a few miles away. We made a lunch time stop at a village pub on the way, which turned out to be a mistake. Terrible service and some of the food wasn’t up to much at all. Doug and Eileen reckoned they could have soled their shoes with their omelettes! We eventually arrived about half hour late for the wine tasting but we weren’t the only ones.

Sunday morning was an even shorter drive to Michelham Priory, a very peaceful and picturesque place. The building which was once an Augustinian Priory is surrounded by the largest water filled mediaeval moat in England. Along with attractive gardens there is also a working watermill, which was the backdrop for a performance by some morris dancers.

Back to the hotel for lunch, which we seemed to have to wait a long time for but the reason appeared to be staffing problems. The kitchen door suddenly opened and a girl shouted out that there were cockroaches in the kitchen and we would all get food poisoning. This was her revenge for being sacked, along with her brother (one of the chefs) when he was caught stealing about £50 worth of steak! It happened to be Eileen’s birthday and Eric and Margot had arranged for a cake to be presented to her at the table.

The majority of people headed home in various directions after Sunday lunch but the six of us along with about ten others were staying on an extra night. With some time to kill on Monday morning before hitting the M25 we visited a llama breeding centre before lunch in Hartfield (Winnie the Pooh territory) at a very good pub this time. We always enjoy these weekends but it was nice to have a much shorter journey than the last two years to Dartmoor and N. Yorkshire.
