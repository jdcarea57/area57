Newsletter - December 2003

Area Rep’s Christmas Message

Doesn’t time fly when you’re having fun? Every time I write a Christmas message its as if I’ve only just finished the last one.

Another busy year nearly over, another Enfield Pageant bites the dust, What a great time we have there, and once again I must thank everyone for the immense amount of hard work that goes into making our stand look so good.

Lets hope that the Cricketers at Rickling Green will still be available for our Breakfast Runs in April and October next year as they are always so well supported and enjoyed by everyone, and don’t they just get better every time? It will be a real shame if we have to try and find another venue that looks after us so well in such a pretty village.

Christmas Dinner at our new venue the Old Manor in Potters Bar was a great night out with lovely surroundings, good food and a nice atmosphere.

We’ve had some new shows to go to this year in some very nice areas, lets hope for more of the same in the new year.

I’ve been fairly busy with rehearsals for "Guys & Dolls" recently but Wendy and Barb have been keeping things ticking over for me, and we’ll soon be back to normal in March after the show. Remember the dates for your diary: Monday March 8th to Saturday March 13th at Saffron Walden Town Hall. I look forward to seeing you there.

Remember my plea every year? the same one, please send Wendy articles for the newsletter. Cast your mind back to those long hot sunny days and send her something that hasn’t been featured already. I did hear a vague little story about Brian Kingsley and Ron Risk the other night, perhaps someone would care to enlighten us on that one!

Finally, my personal thanks to all of you for your continued help and support over the past year. Merry Christmas and safe motoring in 2004.

Alan

CHRISTMAS DINNER - Saturday 13th December

The Old Manor at Potters Bar is the sort of building usually found on Christmas cards, surrounded by snow and with a coach and horses outside. Inside everything looked very welcoming and Christmassy. Our tables were upstairs in the gallery area and the service was all pretty efficient. I can only speak for those on our table but the opinions of the food and general ambience was favourable.

There are a couple of chances to blow away the cobwebs during the holiday period; the EDVVS run to the Sun at Nazing on 27th December and the gathering at the White Horse, Shenley on New Year’s Day.  The New Year’s Day meeting always attracts a large number of Bentleys and other very elderly cars that make ours look like mere youngsters.
