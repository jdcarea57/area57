Newsletter - August 2005
 

Let me start by thanking Keith and Audrey for their hospitality on the occasion of "Keith’s 60th Birthday Bash". Cool but dry weather helped towards making it a successful evening, as did the live music. Many of you are familiar with the white sports shirt that Alan has been trying to get rid of for a couple of years. He gift wrapped it for Keith but may well get it back again when his birthday comes around. Thanks from Keith for any Cancer Research donations, and desserts, cakes etc.

I mentioned last time that Len and Sally were going to try and book a breakfast for us at The Woodman, Nuthampsted (this side of Royston) on September 4th. Those of you going should already know the details. The cost is £7.50 per head and the meeting points are the normal ones at Bullsmoor Lane , Enfield & Royal Forest Hotel, Chingford leaving at 8 a.m.

The previous day several of us will be at Buntingford. Anyone who has not booked for this should ring the organiser, Leigh Wood (01763- 274743) to see if places are still available.

Although there has been some nice weather this summer, the Southend Run and Harpenden certainly didn’t have any luck with the sunshine.

XK Day at Burghley House on 14th August was dry luckily. Like most clubs and Areas, the XK committee realise that there are rarely as many cars in attendance as there used to be, so sent flyers to other car clubs and it was nice to see some other cars (M.G’s, Mercedes, Austin Healey) amongst the Jaguars. As usual we had driven up on Friday afternoon to be ready for a drive around on Saturday morning. We had a few showers which settled into steady rain after we arrived at our hotel, the Ramada at Peterborough. This was very close to the River Nene and overlooked a 1,000 metre competition rowing course. That weekend it was the venue for a big regatta and trailer loads of rowing boats started arriving that evening. Saturday morning was dry but overcast and rather than visit a farm centre which we had already seen before we went to the pretty little market town of Oundle. As we left there after lunch, the rain started and by the time we were due to visit Marshalls, the main Jaguar dealer at their new showroom it was pouring. We were made very welcome with tea and cake etc., and several people took the opportunity to have a sit in the new XK8.

Eventually the sun came out just as we assembled for a Bucks Fizz in the evening and an hours boat trip along the Nene before dinner. This was very pleasant on a covered boat with a bar but it started raining a little bit on the way back. Just before we reached the little landing stage it stopped raining for a few seconds before the heavens opened just as we arrived. We then had to get back to the hotel, three or four minutes walk away, all dressed for dinner! A couple of the staff came to meet us and ran a shuttle service with big umbrellas and we were met with towels by more staff as we got in. The trip had been pleasant but its ending made it unforgettable!

QUOTE;- "Mad dogs and Englishmen go out in the rain as well!"

CLASSICS ON THE COMMON - HARPENDEN - Wednesday 27th July

Report by Keith Williams

It was a very sad and depleted convoy (Len, Sally, Richard and ourselves) who set off from Hertford on a wet, miserable and cold Wednesday afternoon. Surprisingly there were also hundreds of other people just as mad as we were! The cars covered the two commons but we estimated the show was only half its normal size.

Erics gazebo was jam packed with people, wine and food!! Sadly we had to attempt to eat our picnic in the E-type! The chicken Caesar salad I had planned became plain chicken filled rolls - but we did manage to open some wine as well. Quite a balancing act in a two seater! We ambled in the rain to our usual chip shop and enjoyed the freshly cooked chips on the walk back to the show.

An enjoyable but soggy evening.
