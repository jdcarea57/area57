Newsletter - October 2004

It is not often that I can mention a forthcoming event this late in the year. Over the years it has often been suggested that we visit the Stondon Transport Museum in Bedfordshire, but it has never actually happened. However, Dick and Eric recently visited it and we are hoping to go there on Sunday 14th November. Obviously it would be nice if the weather was kind enough to use Jaguars, but that can’t be relied upon. It is proposed that we have an early lunch at the nearby village of Shillington and then visit the museum. Eric has spoken to the landlord of the Crown who said we would be welcome at about 12 to 12.15. There is a lot to see at the Museum which has a collection of over 400 exhibits.

Our thanks to Keith Oswick for organising the trip to Southend on the "Waverly" paddle steamer. We were picked up from Enfield for the coach ride to Tower Bridge, where the boat was moored. Personally, I had never seen Tower Bridge open, so it was nice to be on the boat that it was opening for. Unfortunately it was rather a grey morning but not cold, so most of us stayed on deck for the three hour trip down the Thames with the various places of interest being pointed out by the commentary. For those of a certain age, it was interesting to see that the Radio Caroline boat is now moored on the Thames. Luckily the sun came out while we were at Southend, and after the almost obligatory fish and chips we headed for the funfair. I think Area 57 has been suffering from dodgem withdrawal symptoms this year, so we had a couple of turns on those. Most of the "boys" also had a go on the Go-Kart track, but the lack of driving skill of a little Japanese boy kept bringing everything to a halt. Our coach driver picked us up punctually for the return journey to finish off a really enjoyable day.

On the same day several members of the SS Register made another visit to the Whitewebbs Museum.

Our end of season Breakfast Run went well, the new A10 which had opened two days earlier meant that those of us from the Bullsmoor Lane meeting point had a good run. I think the general opinion was better than the April breakfast which was the first time the new owner had catered for us. As usual thanks to Barbara for organising it and the tea and coffee afterwards.

Earlier in the year I mentioned a couple of dates at the Ace Cafe. Since then, Eric Dick and Brian seem to have become regular visitors and apparently on a recent wet evening, there were about forty classic cars there, including some quite unusual vehicles.
