Newsletter - October 2005
 

Many of you will be aware from the JDC Magazine that the Jaguar Drivers’ Club is celebrating fifty years next May. We at Area 57, also have an important anniversary in March as we will reach our twenty years This is an achievement that we intend to celebrate in some way and any ideas are welcome. Some of us were talking about it at the last meeting and various suggestions have been made already. On our tenth anniversary we celebrated at the Whitewebbs Museum, bringing in our own buffet. This could be repeated, although having caterers supply the food would be an alternative. Our venue for the Christmas meal, Pearse House could also be suitable. Other suggestions have been a drive to a suitable pub for a lunch, a trip along the River Lea on a dining cruise. A few of us recently had a day in York with a first class train trip which included full breakfast and four course dinner. This was very enjoyable but dates and destinations for next year are only just becoming available.

In past Newsletters, both Alan and I have mentioned that there are a lot of regular members on our list who we never see. It is gratifying that you feel that the Newsletter keeps you in touch but on the occasion of our twentieth anniversary, it really would be nice to meet up with our not so familiar members.

That view also applies to the Christmas Dinner on 3rd December. Everyone should now have a copy of the menu and we hope that more of you will give Barbara a ring to book. She will need a deposit of £5 a head and your choices from the menu. Alan assures us that the food is very good and plentiful.

At the end of September, Doug and I joined up with the XK register for a weekend in Hampshire. We were staying at a conference centre near Haselmere and it was a much earlier breakfast than some people are used to on a Saturday morning before organiser Nick Peace’s briefing and our drive to Portsmouth. He had arranged for our cars to be parked in front of H.M.S. Victory. He had to provide names and car registrations beforehand for security reasons. He also had to provide us all with a large piece of cardboard to stop oil drips on quay area. However, on a fairly windy day these didn’t stay in position very well. We could have done with some of the Victory’s cannon balls to weigh them down. WE had day tickets to enjoy any of the numerous attractions on offer; the Victory, Mary Rose, Mary Rose Museum and the Warrior which was part of the transition between sail and steam. We also had a boat trip around the naval dockyard with an informative commentary. It would probably take two days to really see everything that there is available.

On Sunday morning in glorious sunshine we visited Bignor Roman Villa near Petworth. There are many fine examples of their mosaic floors including one almost complete corridor floor. The site is well away from main road along twisting country lanes. We opted to just follow two other cars who knew the right way. Unfortunately we lost sight of them around the bends and at a junction had to make our own decision.

I soon realised that we weren’t on the same roads as before, but by this time we had a procession of about six Xks and an E-type behind us! I’m used to Dick and Eric’s cars following us and accepting any of my navigation errors, but this number of cars was somewhat different. However, it all worked out OK and we arrived at our Sunday lunch destination. It was a very well organised weekend and the lovely weather was a real bonus.

I should have mentioned last month that the saga of Alan’s white shirt continued when Doug "won" it in a quiz while we were away at Tewkesbury.

It is the time of the year for British Legion Memberships to be renewed, as regular visitors should all be members there. There may be an odd occasion when the main bar is in use due to a quiz night. If you arrive at any time and find that this is the case, we will be upstairs.

It has been suggested that maybe we could make the Christmas meal a fancy dress. We talked about it at the last meeting and some of the reaction was "I will if you will" but in general I think the opinion was that fancy dress would be better for a dinner/dance/disco rather than just a meal. All views are welcome, as they are about anything else to do with the club.
