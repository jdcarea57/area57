Eric and Keith W. have both been keeping me supplied with newspaper/magazine articles. I’ll try and précis pieces like this but will always have the relevant articles with me if anyone wants to read them properly.

From The Times - One of only four D-types left in immaculate condition was being auctioned at Coys this month and expected to fetch £2 million plus. It had been sold for £2,500 when it finished racing after finishing fourth in two Le Mans when driven by Paul Frere and Jacques Swaters.

From the Sunday Times - Motoring correspondent Andrew Frankel was extremely impressed with the latest XK12 coupe and during the three days in which he had it, took every opportunity to take it out, and found it a perfect blend of point to point efficiency and character. The XKR shows that whatever the trouble at the top of Jaguar, the firm still has enough engineering talent to make up in clear thinking and inspired design what the company lacks in cash. Indications are demand will outstrip supply and it has been announced that the XKR will race at international level next year.

The article that Keith passed to me is about someone having a bespoke MKII to all his own requirements including a 4.7 litre engine instead of 3.8. It doesn’t really lend itself to shortening this article but, as I mentioned, it will be in my case at club meetings if anyone wants to have a look.

I think most of us have found that our lives go in different phases over the years and thirty years ago Doug was very much involved with model engineering and built a small traction engine, two sixth scale ploughing engines and a quarter scale steam lorry. He recently decided to get the lorry running again to show our grandchildren. Before putting it away again for the winter, he mentioned at a club meeting that if anyone wanted to see it in steam they were welcome. As a result about thirteen people turned up on a Sunday morning to see it steaming. Unfortunately, because of very uneven paving due to tree roots, a chain broke but everyone seemed to find it interesting. It was such a lovely morning that we were able to have tea, coffee and cakes outside at the same time. I gather from Roy that there is now a picture of the lorry on our club website.

Bentley African Tour

Richard Freestone’s Diary

Greetings from Graaf Reinet, Eastern Cape,

Been away for over 2 weeks now and having a wonderful time. We started in Ballito Bay in a real 5* place, Zimbali Lodge. Glad to get there as flight delayed ( 3 hours on the tarmac at Heathrow. Long haul is really the pits these days!

Played golf on a very difficult course with loads of trees and undergrowth and between the four of us lost 25 balls!!

Collected the car form Durban docks on 28/9. Everything fine - all cars started. On 30/9 we headed for Mkuze. Pleasant drive thru great scenery to Ghost Mountain Inn. Went on two game drives but most animals in hiding. Saw Impala, gnu, kudu, heron, spoonbill, hippo & crocs at a distance but no elephant or lion. On Monday 2/10 we set off for Swaziland. Delay at border but no real problems. Arrive at Piggs Peak hotel where Annie is waiting for me. Great drive in beautiful weather but next morning it's misty, cold & wet. My mate Doug Salmon ( Speed 6) has broken a back spring taking a speed hump too fast. ( they are even more prolific than back home but many have no warnings). Spent most of the day helping him to get it fixed at a local agric machinery garage where they had all the heavy welding gear. On Weds 4/10 we set off for Natal Spa, Paul Pietersberg. Decided to have country walk with 5 others to alleviate some of the excesses of the last 10 days or so. All but me and one other slipped on stepping stones over a stream and ended up covered in black mud. Nobody injured & very amusing for the rest of us. Done about 800 miles at that stage and B going well but doesn't like altitiudes ( 8000 ft highest so far). Fuel about 45p per litre and making about 13 mpg. On from there to Drakensberg mountains where we saw just about the best display of falconry ever . Eagles, hawks, vultures. Played pitch & putt on a 6 hole coures and managed to lose 8 balls between the two of us!

Next day we left for Lesotho. Quite a long drive with a very strong headwind in the morning. B running very rich and lack of power resulting in only 10 mpg. Weaken up the mixture after lunch and do a little better bur still not running right. The tour mechanics attend to it the next day and diagnose worn carburettor jets but do the best they can and full power retruned but still only 12 mpg-- good job the fuel's cheap here!! Job for when it's back home. On to Lesotho ( forgotten Kingdom). Extremely poor country. Weather really bad here. Almost continuous rain. Went out for lunch in back of Chris Wray's 4.5 and got drowned coming back in hail/thunderstorm. Now back in civilisation, Graaf Reinet and weather thankfully improved. many of the tour group have suffered with a two day flu thing but Annie & I OK.

Hope y'all are well and will update you when I return home on 24/10.

Best,

Richard/ Ferd

Bentley African Tour - Part 2

Greetings from home,

Arrived back Tuesday last week and have to admit it's been a bit tough getting back to normal.

Picking up from the last newsletter;

Having left Graaff Reinet, the next destination was a one night stop in Cradock a small town steeped in Victoriana. Overall the impression was a pretty poor area with vast unemployment amongst whites as well as blacks. However the accommodation was delightfully quaint in the form of self contained time warp cottages. That evening we were entertained in the local church by traditional African singing and dancing and a boys school choir. Next day an early start for a relatively long drive to Shamwari, the last 10 miles or so on a dirt road. Took it very steady as didn't want to break springs or spokes. This place is a complete paradise consisting of realtively small game reserve with all the big six present, lion, leopard, elephant, hippo, rhino, giraffe + many others. It was a bit contrived as you're virtually guaranteed to see everything. Each ranger is equipped with network radio so they can alert each other when there is a "major sighting". The lions we saw were so close that as they sauntered past our Land Rover we could have reached out and stroked them ( not recommended )! We saw two Prides with cubs and it was really spectacular to say nothing of the elephants, giraffes, rhino all with calves. Fantastic! And so was the hotel - an all inclusive resort with luxurious apartments. The cost included 4 game drives and all food/ drink. Apparently the cost was £600 per couple per night. Very expensive but worth it if you want to see game. Next a short drive to Port Elizabeth where we had another two day stay. Nothing much to report here. Just a relaxing couple of days, serviced the car and swam in Indian Ocean.

From there we had a longish drive to Knysna. A beautiful place where we had evening cruise/ dinner and some good old pirate songs. Chris Wray snapped his prop shaft but fortunately the tour organise, John White had a spare which was couriered overnight from Cape Town. Next we were on to Majiesfontein a staging post for the Blue train years ago. The hotel was called the Lord Milner. Again it was splendid Victoriana if a little run down at the edges but what atmosphere! There was a real boozers pub to which most had repaired by about 5-00pm as it had been a longish hot drive. Before long one of the staff started playing the piano and there was a singing riot up to dinner then more afterwards. A really great night of over indulgence for which I suffered aver the next 48 hours! So on to our final destination, Cape Town, where for a horrible hour or so I couldn't find my passport. After phoning the last few hotels to no avail we were just about to contact the British Consulate when Annie suddenly recalled she'd noticed it on the mantelpiece in the room on the morning of our departure from the Lord Milner at Majiesfontein when I was speaking to God on the big white telephone and stowed it in a pocket in her suitcase. What a relief. This is a very prosperous place with no end of building going on. Table mountain was spectacular but unfortunately Annie had to leave on Saturday evening to be back in time for work on Monday. It was great to meet up with Julia Howard whom those from EY might remember. Tall, beautiful blonde lady from Cape Town who worked alongside John Elder on 5th Floor Lintas House for a couple of years before returning to Cape Town in late 02. Julia has since married and has a 4 month old son, Tristram. I met her at breakfast on the Sunday which certainly started tongues wagging! Later that day drove to Cape Point and Cape of Good Hope ( managed to shoo the tourists away so I could get a good picture of the Bentley next to the sign) prior to all meeting up for a riotous lunch at Camps Bay, the home of the tour organiser. The farewell party on Sunday night was great fun and included Chris Wray, Marshall Bailey and several of the girls performing a hilarious song and dance routine about the tour, costumes and all. On Monday we drove the cars in to their containers and after fond and sad farewells set off for home.

That's all folks!

For the anoraks, did 2510 miles averaging just under 13 mpg ( it should do a bit more than this and will have to overhaul the carburettors when it gets back). Used/lost about a quart of oil and no radiator water.

best wishes,

Richard/Ferd
