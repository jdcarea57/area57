Newsletter - November 2003

A few months ago, it was mentioned in the XK News Section of Jaguar Driver that you could apply to the Fee paying Section at DVLA to enquire about previous owners of your vehicle for a £5 charge. I sent off £10 to get details of Doug’s XK and E-type. Eventually a thick envelope came back. There were several pages for each vehicle; however for the XK there was nothing before Doug bought it in 1967 with no reason given for lack of any further information. A barely legible photo copy did show previous owners of the E-type. As none of the telephone choices at the DVLA were applicable, I wrote back to try and find out why there was not further information about the XK. The response has been absolutely nothing, so if you do try this, don’t hold out too much hope.

5th North Wiltshire Tour and Discovery Trail, 11th and 12th October 2003

Report by Simon Waldram

We have always enjoyed actively using our Daimler 2.5 rather than cosseting it, or just attending shows, so over the years we have taken part in many events, some several times: e.g. the Footman James Retrorun, the Haynes 2 day Classic, the London to Brighton run, the Norwich Union (and then Guardian) 3 day Classic, etc. Many of these have been great fun and taken us to new and different places so we tend to keep our eyes open for different events.

This year I received details of the 5th North Wiltshire Tour and Discovery Trail and was surprised to have never heard of it before: how had 4 passed by unnoticed? This is organised by part of the North Wiltshire District Council with the intention of promoting tourism in the area but is a thoroughly professional event. This year’s theme was "Hauntings and Happenings." The entry fee of £180 per car with 2 adults seems quite steep at first sight but when you think what is included it actually is good value for money: route information for 2 full days in the form of "tulip" notes, free admission to all the places visited, morning and afternoon refreshments each day, lunch each day and a superb waitress service formal dinner on the Saturday evening followed by a firework display and dancing to a live band. Oh, and I nearly forgot a major attraction - 6 laps (many actually had more) of Castle Combe track to finish off with. No better way to blow the cobwebs away.

We drove down from London early on Saturday morning and joined the other 74 cars (ranging in age from a 1929 Austin to a 2002 Mini Cooper Clubsport, with a variety of Allards, Bentleys, Jaguars, Morgans in between, plus even a Roller, Ferrari and other fast modern machinery such as Porches, a TVR, etc.) at Hartham Park, near Corsham. Our route then took us to the National Trust village of Laycock and on to Chippenham, Corsham, the Starfall Pottery, Castle Combe Village and the Rattlebone inn at Sherston. At most of the towns we were greeted by the mayor and presented with small gifts (fudge, pens, etc.) We also had check points and an ongoing quiz with answers that had to be found at our stopping points. At each check point and for each correct quiz answer we were given a piece of a jigsaw. By the end of the weekend the idea was that each participant would have a complete, and specially commissioned "5th North Wiltshire Tour and Discovery Trail" jigsaw. We, and many other participants, managed to succeed in this objective. Oh yes, and one more thing: for those who wanted there were driving skills tests to take at various places along the route. We choose to do this but won no prizes: too much enthusiasm and too little accuracy.

We visited many other places including Westonbirt Arboretum, Kemble Airfield and the Bristol Aero Collection, Minchinghampton Common, Keynes Country Park – and that was day just on the first day. On Sunday we enjoyed Calne, the Atwell Wilson Motor Museum (quite extraordinary if you have never been there), Avebury, Butterfly World, Wooton Bassett and had Sunday lunch at the Blunsden House Hotel. The last stages took us through Cricklade, Malmesbury, Grittleton School and finally to Castle Combe for some tyre warming fun.

At the end of Sunday there were many awards and happy smiling faces. The only bad bit was the return journey to London via a heavily congested M4. Including the outward and return legs to London we covered 466 miles so if you only cover a hundred miles a year in your Jaguar this is not the weekend for you! Would the 6th tour be a good event for Area 57 to plan to have several cars at? If so book the dates now: 9th and 10th October 2004. And we found a very good local B&B for Saturday night if any of you are interested.

P.S. some of you were understandably a bit puzzled at the Kelvedon Hatch nuclear bunker visit when I kept blowing our horns. Unfortunately there was an intermittent short of the horn wiring inside the steering column and every time we made a right hand turn the horns would blow! All very embarrassing, particularly on roundabouts so we eventually had to disconnect them.
