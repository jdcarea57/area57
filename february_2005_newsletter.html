Newsletter - February 2005
 

Thanks to Dick, I have got details of several museums that could be of interest to visit at some point in the future. The best known of these is the Heritage Motor Museum at Gaydon, Warwickshire www.heritage-motor-centre.co.uk . The Muckleburgh Collection of Military Vehicles www.muckleburgh.co.uk is at Holt, Norfolk. Closer at hand are The Oxford Bus Museum www.oxfordbusmuseum.org.uk  near Witney, Oxfordshire and the Chiltern Museum of Motoring near Aylesbury. The one that seems to have the widest appeal is again in Norfolk at Downham Market, so not as far as the one at Holt. The Collectors World of Eric St John Foti www.collectors-world.org has an exhibition of Victorian England’s Shops, sights and sounds as well as a collection of Armstrong Siddely cars, carts, carriages, household and farming memorabilia, a Nelson room, a Barbara Cartland room, the Swinging Sixties, radios, dolls and dolls prams and a telephone museum.

I asked at the Gunpowder Mills, Waltham Abbey, www.royalgunpowdermills.com a couple of years ago about group booking but at that time they were only mid week. However, I have followed up this enquiry after Dick passed on an up to date leaflet and group discounts are available at weekends when all their special events are held in addition to the normal exhibits. They are holding a 60th Anniversary of VE Day on 7th and 8th May but there are various events throughout the year.

Doug recently raised the possibility of a visit to the Shuttleworth Collection www.shuttleworth.org near Biggleswade, so I have been finding out a bit more about that. Some interest was expressed in the Military Pageant Air Display on Sunday 5th June. Apparently they never have final confirmation of participating aircraft until a week or two before hand but they would be first and second world war planes and possibly some foreign ones as well. Although we would be able to book as a group and have an allocated space they can’t offer discount on the £15 per adult price on these occasions. the reason being that they have to rope off an area, have directions to it etc. Space is always at a premium at these events so they would have to be paid a couple of weeks in advance as they have been let down in the past by group booking a space for fifty, having only twenty turn up, leaving members of the public not able to get in. You may have noticed in the Jaguar Driver that XJ Day is being held there on 26th June but nothing is flying on that day.

The club has been sent the date of a British Sports Car Day at Brooklands www.brooklandsmuseum.com on 17th July, a change from their previously advertised date of 19th June.

One other date I have been given by Richard is on 3rd July for a fete at Bramfield near Hertford, which he says has been enjoyable in the past.

I will finish this month with a medical bulletin. Brian Kingsley and Ron Risk are both progressing well and are back at club meetings and Ron tells me that Pam is not using sticks anymore. At the time that these three were having operations, I had not realised Karen Smart was having her share of medical problems. A long term kidney stone problem has yet to be resolved but Karen has now been able to return to work. I hope it all gets sorted eventually and that everyone continues to make good progress.

THE ACE CAFE RUN, PARK ROYAL

Report by Richard Rogers

On Tuesday 8th Feb. myself, Eric and Keith went to the Classic Car Evening at the famous Ace Cafe www.ace-cafe-london.com . I took the MK2. We had a nice meal and were surprised at the amount of classics that turned up, ranging from MGs of the ‘60s going back as far as a 1926 Morris Commercial truck belonging to a London College.

The highlight of the evening was a Le Mans type Jaguar racing car.

The event takes place on the 2nd Tuesday of the month all year round. If anybody wants to go next month we leave my house about 6.30 p.m. or we meet there.

My pride & joy...and I am going to raffle it

Father donates classic Jag to charity that helped him over son’s death

From The Mail On Sunday 13/02/2005

When Peter Sidebottom's only son was killed in a car crash, it was months before he could bear to look at the classic Jaguar they had been restoring together. But five years on, with refurbishment of the £70,000 1951 XK120 coupe complete, Mr Sidebottom is raffling it for charity. The 62-year-old property developer from Wiltshire is hoping to raise £250,000 for the Child Bereavement Trust, which provides counselling for parents after a tragedy. About 125,000 tickets at £2 each are being sold and the draw takes place on May 8 at the Badminton horse trials in Gloucestershire. The Child Bereavement Trust is the event's nominated charity for this year.

Mr Sidebottom said: "Harry's two passions in life were horses and sports cars. It seems a fitting memorial to him to raise money for a bereavement charity by raffling the very special car we were building together for him."

Harry, 24, was killed on September 5,1999, near the family home. Mr Sidebottom had to break the news to his ex-wife Flappy - Harry's mother - who was abroad at the time. He said: "Even at the awful time I was being told about Harry's death, I was struck by how sensible, calm and instructive the young police constables were. I was to learn later that they had been schooled by the Child Bereavement Trust."

Mr Sidebottom and his former wife became deeply involved in the charity's work, with Harry's mother raising £600,000 to set up and run its bereavement helpline in 2001. Money raised from the car raffle will keep the helpline staffed for a further two to three years. Mr Sidebottom added: "The charity saved our lives after Harry was killed, it gave us hope. Now I want to help others."
