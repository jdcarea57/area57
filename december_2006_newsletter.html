Newsletter - December 2006
 
 

Area Rep’s Christmas Message

This time last year I was sending you a report on all the 3’s. 3 major hospital operations, 3 major birthdays, 3 breakfast runs. Little did I know that there would be more 3’s in stock for this year, and I would be at the centre of them. First the accident, then the stroke, and just to round it off, a big dose of septicaemia, so believe it or not, I personally will be very glad to see the back of 2006.

I would just like to say a big ‘Thank You’ to you all for your very kind wishes throughout this, and the support you gave to Barb.

As you all know, I’m fit and well again, (despite Saturday 2nd December) and am looking forward to a fresh new year.

A club like ours cannot survive 20 years without the support of its membership, and because of this we have continued to grow. We may have had a number of disappointments because of the weather this year, but some new shows have helped us to see new places and new faces.

Let’s keep our fingers crossed for 2007 and see if we can get back to some good sunshine and be able to enjoy our hobby even more.

Let me just say again a great big ‘Thank You’ for your support and help over the past year, and here’s hoping for a bright new healthy 2007.

Wishing you a very Merry Christmas.

Alan

This is something of a milestone, as in all the time that Roy, Kim and I have been producing the Newsletter this will be the first time that Kim hasn't had to decifer my handwriting first, as we have arrived in the world of E mail at last.

On Saturday 2nd December just over thirty of us enjoyed a very good evening at Pearse House where we held our Christmas Dinner for the second year. No dressing up this time, but Eric &Margot had organised a sing song of well known Christmas songs. It was without doubt a successful occasion; good food, good company in pleasant surroundings and, on one particular table , wine was flowing well !!!!!

There will be two chances to use the cars over the holiday period; the EDVVS will make their usual run to The Sun at Nazeing on Wednesday 27th December, and the Bentley and Vintage Sports Car Club will meet at The Pre Hotel near St Albans towards Redbourn, on New Year's Day at around mid day. Last year there was a very good selection of vehicles so well worth a drive out if you need to get rid of some cobwebs.

Although the next meeting falls on New Year'sDay some of us will be there as usual .
