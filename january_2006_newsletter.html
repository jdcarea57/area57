Newsletter - January 2006
 
I’ll start by wishing you all a Happy New Year and apologising for not giving earlier notice for an outing that Keith Oswick is organising for us on 6th February. There is a new motorway control centre at South Mimms (J23 of the M25) and Keith has made arrangements for us to go there at 8.00 p.m on Monday 6th February. This would normally be a club night so please don’t go to the Legion that evening. On reaching J23 follow signs to the Services area and then park in the area for caravans.

There have been enough people at the last couple of meetings to discuss the Club’s 20th anniversary celebrations. As you know from the last couple of newsletters, several ideas have been suggested, however we have eventually decided on a party at Pearse House (where we had our Christmas dinner) to be held on the evening of Saturday 25th March. It was felt that it would be better to have somewhere exclusively for our use, as several people have said that they would like to be able to browse the Club photo albums, which we would be unable to do if we were amongst a lot of other people. At Pearse House we will also be able to show videos taken during the early years of Area 57. Foodwise, a running buffet was thought preferable to a "sit down dinner", as this would allow people to arrive when they like rather than at a specific time. On this occasion, it really would be nice to meet up with some of our less regular members whose names are familiar to us, but their faces less so. We’ll also be inviting some of our ex-members. The only cost involved will be for your drinks, as the Club will be paying for the venue & food. Although it is going to be an informal affair, Alan needs to have some idea of numbers, so please contact him. Pearse House at Bishops Stortford is easy to find, and directions will be included with the next newsletter.

I’m sure most of you are aware that the JDC is celebrating 50 years in May and we hope to be able to arrange a space for our Area at Heythrop Park near Chipping Norton on 21st May. We haven’t been to many JDC events as a club lately so it’s a good opportunity to celebrate their anniversary as well as our own.

A few months back, I wrote that we do not seem to go to so many events en masse as we used to. This was emphasised by an old newsletter that Dick came across and it makes you realise that many of the old venues have now been discontinued. For many years we went to the Cancer Research Show in September at the Bald Faced Stag and also the MayDay Show at Stanborough Lakes. Neither of these now take place, and others, like the MG Club show at Chigwell, have deteriorated. Can I ask that this year, if you know of a show that you feel is worth a visit (particularly those that are not just car shows), please let Alan or myself know, so that we can publicise it and ascertain if anyone else would like to attend.

Barbara has booked the first Breakfast Run of the season to the Cricketers as usual, on Sunday 9th April, and has started collecting names. Call her for bookings.

Several people who were at Mince Pie night got involved in the quiz that Alan had from work. The joint brain power was very successful and his was the winning entry.

This year’s A.G.M. will be on Monday, 20th March so start thinking who you think you’ll vote for to receive the John Dyall trophy.

Dick has given me the information about the charity show in aid of the Little Haven Children’s Hospice on Sunday 20th August at Althorne, Nr. Burnham-on-Crouch. Those who went last year really enjoyed it and obviously it’s for a very good cause. I expect that Dick will collect names nearer the time. He has also given me the date for a show a the Museum of Power at the Steam Pumping Station at Maldon on Sunday 11th June. I believe some people went to this show a couple of years ago.
