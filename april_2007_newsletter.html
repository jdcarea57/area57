Newsletter - April 2007
 
The first April meeting was Doug's first as Area Rep, and he started by thanking Alan for his fifteen years service in the position. When Alan took over from Chris Engeham there were three volunteers and a vote was held, a luxury that we have not had since. As I mentioned last month we now intend to make the post a three year tenure.

The first Breakfast Run of the season was very enjoyable, a lovely summer's day in the middle of April can't be bad, although there was early morning fog in the area near the pub. The Bullsmoor Lane contingent had to make an early detour as the A10 was cordoned off by police, presumably due to an accident. As Roy was using his motorbike, it looked as though we had our own outrider.

The April 16th meeting was the first time that we had held a Quiz Night, and it was good to see some of our lady members there; the men needed the extra brain power. Doug and I had never organised a quiz before, so we hope everyone enjoyed it. Thirty questions were General Knowledge and twenty one were motoring and Jaguar. Interestingly, there was only a spread of thirteen points between the lowest score and the winner, who was Roy.

By the time of the next meeting on Bank Holiday Monday, 7th May, eight of our members will have been on a weekend to Portsmouth with the EDVVS and Alan & Barb are joining us on an XK weekend to Cheshire.

There is an interesting meeting at Silverstone over that weekend. France brought details for us but if you haven't seen them, everything is on the Silverstone website.

The May newsletter will probably not be circulated before the Enfield Pageant, so just a reminder to be on site by no later than 9 a.m. and if you are normally responsible for bringing anything, don't forget. A reminder that tea, coffee, bacon rolls etc. are provided, but mugs are not. Any queries at all about the Pageant, ring Eric.

Looking ahead a bit further to some other events that have been recommended in the past by members. Now that I have joined the wonderful world of Google, I have been checking some of them out. The first of these is the Luton Festival of Transport on 10th June. Eric has been to this in the past and last year they had 1100 vehicles and fifty clubs. Pre-booked entries are free for driver and passenger or £3 on the day. There is free entry to the Vauxhall Heritage Centre.

I was often told that the Kimbolton Country Fayre was a very good show so I've looked that one up as well. It is on July 15th and organised by the Sporting Bears. I have seen a long list of the attractions that were there last year including 700 classic cars. It does sound a similar show to how Chigwell used to be. Kimbolton is a little to the west of the A1 just past St Neots. If there is any interest I will check out the details nearer the time.

Those of you who receive the newsletter by E-mail will have seen the details that David Holland sent us about a show on 5th August at Helvingham Hall in Suffolk, which like the Kimbolton Show may be too far for some, but could also be a nice day out, after all quite a few of us used to go as far as Bressingham for East Anglian Day. There were over 400 cars there last year. I have a phone contact (01473-890799) but if you are able to follow up the information on David's E-mail or go directly to www.helminghamhall.com all the details about various pre-booking can be found there.

At the meeting on the 16th we discussed the possibility of having a Sunday lunch at the Woodman where we go for our Breakfast runs; it had been suggested by the lady there. Barb is going to follow this up for us to see what they would do for what price and to check dates. Hopefully more details next time.

The idea of a boat trip has also been raised and Keith O is going to see what he can find out for us. Another idea, which would probably have to be done at short notice is a club picnic; suggestions for a suitable venue would be welcome.

Have you started thinking about Christmas yet? Alan found that some bookings had already been made at Pearse House so with the agreement of those present at the last meeting he has booked us in on Saturday 8th December.

Have you sent for the free DVD from JDC yet? We have only seen some of it so far but it looks to be a good record of the Club's history. As our car is the first one seen, perhaps we can be forgiven a little prejudice.

Don't forget to keep an eye on the calendar in the Jaguar Driver as well as our own.
