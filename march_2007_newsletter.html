Newsletter - March 2007
 

Monday 19th March - Minutes of the 21st A.G.M.

Twenty people were at the A.G.M. this year, five more than last year. Alan thanked everyone for coming and it was agreed by everyone present that the minutes of last year's meeting should be taken as read.

Area Rep's Report

Alan went through the various events and places visited over the past year. The highlight was our party at Pearce House to celebrate the Area's 20th birthday, when it was good to see not only some of our less regular members but some ex-members as well. It was a very enjoyable evening with videos and photo albums of the Club over those twenty years. We had three Breakfast Runs throughout the year; the first in April to the Cricketer's at Rickling Green, then both the mid season and end of the season runs were to The Woodman at Nuthampstead which has proved to be a very popular venue. What should have been the two biggest events of the year were both washouts unfortunately. The Enfield Pageant had to be cancelled for only the second time in its history because the ground was almost unusable due to the heavy rain in the days leading up to the weekend. The same fate befell the J.D.C. 50th celebrations which went ahead in appalling conditions. As usual we went to two town centre shows; Hoddesdon and Buntingford with Keith and Audrey providing tea and cakes in their garden after the first of these. Harpenden was the usual success with hundreds of cars of all ages shapes and sizes. Another show rather like a small Harpenden was the one at Croxley Green; again there was a good range of vehicles plus the added attraction of Carter's Steam Fair with their excellent dodgems. The show near Burnham on Crouch in aid of the Little Haven children’s’ hospice was an enjoyable event with plenty to see as well as cars. Dick and Eileen invited us back to their nearby caravan for tea and cakes afterwards. The event raised about £7,000. The Trent Park Show fared better than the Pageant although there were some heavy showers and it was notable as the last occasion that we enjoyed Dave Norton's commentary before he and Penny emigrated to Australia. There are usually some weekends away throughout the year and although we had to miss the XK weekend in North Wales, the one incorporating XK Day at Stamford was enjoyable. The EDVVS was in the Bridgenorth area but I gather that the hotel left quite a bit to be desired. For our own Area weekend in Norfolk we were lucky, with very good weather and a comfortable hotel. Throughout the year Dick has been a regular visitor to the Ace Cafe. IT was back to Pearce House again for our Christmas dinner. There are probably several other events that I have not mentioned but Alan's run through gave us a reminder of 2006 which for him had been rather an eventful one as far as health is concerned.

Treasurer's Report

A very healthy Statement of Accounts was presented by Barbara Collins, with the majority of our expenses being fairly consistent from year to year.

Secretary/NewsletterEditor's Report

Wendy Shipman thanked Roy and Kim Fludgate for their work in producing and distributing the Newsletter every month in what has been written in my not very good handwriting until the last few months. We have just about reached the 21st century now and by E-mailing the Newsletter to them they now get it two days earlier. My usual thanks to those who have written anything for me and a request for more write-up's of events that people go to.

Membership Secretary's Report

Roy Fludgate reported that there were seven lapsed memberships this year, mainly people who have moved away or do not have their car any longer. We have a new member, France Aliphon who owns an XJS that used to be his father's. Unfortunately he has a difficult journey to get to meetings. It is not easy to get young people into the Club as these days the prohibitive cost of insurance makes it difficult. We have 37 memberships representing 57 people and 61 cars. There has been regular contact with JDC Editor Steve Fermor as a result of the web site, which still gets plenty of hits.

Election of Officers

It was unanimously agreed that Barbara Collins, Wendy Shipman and Roy Fludgate would carry on as before with Brian Kingsley continuing to act as Club Archivist. For several years Alan has expressed a wish to stand down as Area Rep; understandable after fifteen years service. Doug Shipman has agreed to take over on the firm understanding that it would be for a maximum term of three years, and this is a policy that was agreed for the future. Alan has given a lot of commitment to the Area over the years, which I'm sure is appreciated by us all.

There was nothing raised for discussion under Any Other Business so Alan closed the meeting.

Presentation of the John Dyall Trophy

Valerie Dyall was with us to present the Trophy, which this year went to Tom Jones; a very popular choice.

With the AGM concluded we then began the usual meeting.

There is probably still time to contact Barbara to book for the Breakfast Run on April 15th to the Woodman at Nuthampstead near Royston. If anyone is interested in U.S aircraft from the second World War there is a lot of memorabilia there as well as a good breakfast after a nice early drive. Meeting places are the usual ones at Royal Forest Hotel and opposite MacDonalds in Bullsmoor Lane. Lately not so many people have been meeting at the Royal Forest so if you do intend to go there please let John & Loraine know.

Dick has been given information by Nik Drukker about a charity flying day at RAF Halton near Wendover (not far from Tring) on June 24th. Tiger Moths & early DeHaviland planes will be flying with trips in them available. I believe the cost is £60. Classic cars would have prime parking and could possibly give rides along the runway to people, presumably to also raise money for the children's charity that is being supported. Although this is not until June 24th Dick needs to give some numbers to Nik as soon as possible.

Greenwood Exhibitions are holding shows at Shuttleworth College on May 6th & 7th, Barleylands Showground, Billericay on 14th & 15th July and Knebworth on 26th & 27th August. Dick has given me an entry form for these shows but it does mention that pre booking has to be done 28 days before the event.

I understand that the great potato challenge is on again this year ; if you are a budding spud grower Dick can tell you more.

Thanks to Dick for the following two articles; what a lot of typing practice I'm getting!

A Day at Pinewood Studios - March 1st

By Dick Rogers (Film Star)

At about 6.30 a.m, John Wilkinson in his Mk2 and I went round the M25 to the M40 to go to the studios. I was in my well travelled XK150 but John's car had never been so far from home before. When we arrived we were sent to wardrobe and makeup. I had a suit and tie while John was in casual clothes from the late 60's. We had breakfast and then we were sent to the set, which was a mock up of Baker Street in London in 1971, where a robbery had taken place. There were at least twenty Classics there ranging from vans to an Austin Healey. We were sent up and down a one way three lane road with directions from a young lady. On one run, John's boot lid came undone and was going up and down. By about midday his battery was flat and he went home. I don't know who had been more frightened at being 33 miles from home, John or his car!! I stayed till about 8.30 and had a good run back along the M25. It was a good day, meeting the other owners and getting paid for it. Film work is not to be recommended if you don't like doing a lot of driving. The main actor in the film was David Suchet.

Sunday March 11th

By Dick Rogers

Five members enjoyed doing what we used to do a lot more of; driving our cars, when we took a run up to the Silver Ball cafe near Royston on a sunny Sunday morning. We met at Eric's; Doug in his 120, Ron in his E-type. Unfortunately Keith Oswick's Mk2 had run out of MOT so he travelled down in Ron's car and back in mine. With Eric's 150 completing the group we took it in turns to lead so we could all enjoy the different exhaust sounds. A good time was had by all.
