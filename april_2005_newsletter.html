FOREST FROLICS WEEKEND (Whitewebbs Museum) - Friday 1st April – Sunday 3rd April 2005

Report by Audrey & Keith Williams

We met up with Eileen & Dick at ‘The Bush’ in, a nice little place called, Ovington for a pub lunch about one-ish – too late for Dick to try his April Fool’s joke on us! It was sunny & warm for a change so we took the opportunity of eating outside in a pretty woodland setting. We managed to proceed to our hotel in Brockenhurst without Dick doing his apparent usual detour via Southampton! Once again the Museum members made us very welcome and we had enough time to walk into the village for afternoon tea.

Saturday again was warm & sunny and we visited Sammy Miller’s motorcycle museum in the morning & Beaulieu in the afternoon. They had allocated the arena for our parking – so our collection of about 22 classic cars became one of the attractions!

Saturday evening was something of a gala night with free wine on the tables, a fancy hat competition, live music & dancing and a free raffle. The theme of the hat contest was a film/song or a book. There were some wonderful creations. It kept us amused most of the evening trying to guess the titles. We thought it may be great entertainment value to do something similar for our Club at an event, like the Christmas meal? There might even be enough pennies in the funds to buy some prizes!!

More sunshine on Sunday for our visit to Bucklers Hard Maritime museum. The car park became a source of interest to visitors when another car rally arrived.- the Carpenter’s Arms car club from Harpenden. (what a small world!) We recognised some of their members so had a quick chat about the next display on the Common in July.

We all stopped off about half way home for a Carvery lunch at ‘The Windmill’ in Alton. After the usual prize giving and speeches we headed home, still in glorious sunshine. A wonderful weekend again, made by the good weather and such great value for money. There’s still an open invitation to all our members to take part in EVVVS’ next rally in September, where I believe East Anglia was mentioned as a possible destination.

MESSAGE TO ALL MEMBERS FROM ALAN COLLINS

As most of you know, I have nothing to do with the finances of the Club. Barbara keeps a close eye on everything, the same as at home, but this time she made me look at the cheques received for our annual subscription before she banked them.

The reason for this was because there were so many names that we couldn’t actually put faces to, as well as lots of names of friends that we haven’t seen for quite a while.

I would, therefore, just like to say a sincere "thank you" to all our friends, past and present, that have supported our Area of the Jaguar Drivers’ Club for so many years.

In fact, next year we celebrate the 20th anniversary of Area 57. Nearer to the time, we hope to plan a celebration party, similar to the one we held ten years ago at the Whitewebbs Museum of Transport.

At this time it would be great for everyone to get together again and to renew friendships, and make welcome people normally unable to come to meetings.
