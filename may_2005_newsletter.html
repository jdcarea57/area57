Newsletter - May 2005
 

Just a brief reminder about the Pageant. As usual, please remember to be on site by 9 a.m and leave your keys with someone if you want to go walk about before all the cars are arranged. Bacon rolls, tea and coffee are all provided but please remember to bring a mug.

The Breakfast Run on 24th April went well. Although it wasn’t BBQ weather it was a very pleasant sunny morning. For the first time breakfast was a help yourself buffet; the success or otherwise of this method depended on the time you joined the queue. As usual the morning finished with more tea and coffee at Alan and Barbara’s.

XK OXFORD ODYSSEY 6th - 8th May

Unfortunately, Dick and Eileen couldn’t join us this year, so with just Eric and Margot we headed for Witney, Oxfordshire. As usual there were plenty of familiar cars and faces to meet up with. We were all together for a carvery dinner that evening. On Saturday morning, organiser Brian Davis welcomed everyone before we set off for our first venue; Didcot Railway Centre. Adjacent to the main line station there are plenty of engines and rolling stock to see, both restored and awaiting some TLC from the volunteers that all these railways depend on. En route from here to the next arranged visit we had a very heavy shower, but by the time we reached the Harcourt Arboretum the sun was shining again. This was a very pleasant area to wander around, with a bluebell wood being the highlight. Dinner was due for 8 p.m but the XK Committee had organised pre-dinner drinks without the knowledge of Brian and Mary who have been organising these weekends since about 1993. The new XK Chairman, Mick Duffy thanked them for all their hard work over the years especially as Mary’s health is poor. An engraved glass ornament was presented to them as well as a lovely bouquet for Mary. For once Brian was almost speechless.

On Sunday morning we drove a few miles to the Cotswold Wildlife Park, outside Burford. There are plenty of birds and animals to see, more than we really had time for, all in a lovely parkland setting. The highlight was probably a tiny three day old spider monkey with his or her mum. We had to be back for Sunday lunch at 1.30 p.m so we no doubt missed some things.

After lunch most people were homeward bound although a few had booked an extra night. With the exception of the one shower and some overnight rain, the weather was very kind to us so once again it was an enjoyable weekend., with both cars running well.

As most of you will know, Dick Rogers has been storing and transporting the club tent, banner, and other regalia for the last few years, but he would now like to hand this responsibility over to someone else. The equipment will fit into almost any Jaguar, so if you feel you could take this on please let Alan know.

If anyone is interested in doing the London to Southend Classic Car run on Sunday 24th July, Wayne Blackburn has some booking forms. The entry fee is £12 per car, part of which goes to charity. Please note that this event is open to pre-1976 vehicles only, and the closing date for entries is 11th July.

On the evening of Monday 11th July there will be a visit to the SS Register meeting at The Hollybush, Potters Crouch, near St. Albans. Call Dick Rogers if you are interested in meeting up beforehand.
