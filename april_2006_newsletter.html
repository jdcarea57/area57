Newsletter - April 2006
 
I’ll start this month’s newsletter by thanking Alan and Barbara for organising our 20th Birthday celebrations last month. Nearly fifty of us were there, including some ex-members, some less familiar current members, and one couple joining us for the first time. Pearse House was the perfect venue for the evening as we were free to wander around between the dining room where an excellent buffet was laid out and the entrance hall where a T.V. was set up to show old videos of the club. All the club photo albums were also on display to look through at our leisure. It was a very enjoyable evening.
 
I am sure we think to ourselves that the years have been kind to us but going back twenty years makes you very aware of the growing waist lines, receding hair, lines etc., Later on Doug and I got our own old club videos, mainly copies of the ones that John Ryan had provided for us. One thing that struck us about the early nineties was how busy car shows were then. A show at Essex, I think at Great Leighs, had our cars packed really closely together. Another example was Chigwell, where not only did we have about eighteen cars on display, there were so many more cars there than the show attracts today. We know from XK days that numbers have dwindled and reports of other Jaguar Shows in the Jaguar Driver give the same impression. It seems that in our Area’s early days we were lucky to be part of a very thriving classic car scene. Still after twenty years we are still going strong and we welcomed new members, Albert Scales and Malcolm and Francesca Bowdidge to our last meeting. We just met Albert and his wife Mary at our 20th birthday.
 
We will be booking a space for about fifteen cars at the JDC 50th at Eynsham Park on May 21st. Let Alan or Dick know if you want to attend.
 
A friend of Eric’s has started organising a car gathering at lunchtime on the first Saturday of the month at the Waterend Cafe on the old A1 near Welham Green.
 
Alan has details of the London-Southend Run on 30th July and he is also going to follow up some information that Eric gave him about bowling at Stevenage.
 
The date for the Harpenden "Classics On The Common" Show is Wednesday 26th July, but remember that that only pre-booked entries will be allowed in.
 
Don’t forget that Eric will soon want to collect names for the Enfield Pageant Of Motoring.
 
BREAKFAST RUN - The Cricketers, Rickling Green - Sunday 9th April>
 
Although numbers were a bit lower than usual due to people being away, nearly thirty of us enjoyed the first Breakfast Run of the season on 9th April. It was a crisp but bright morning so it was a good drive and as usual we finished up at Alan and Barbara’s afterwards where we enjoyed some more of the cake that Eileen had made for our party. Looking at the old Videos, I see that some of our regular breakfasters then are still members, so why not join us next time.
