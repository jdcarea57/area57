Newsletter - June 2006
 
As there was a good turnout at the first June meeting, we took the opportunity to sort out a date for a mid-season Breakfast Run. This will be on Sunday 16th July to the Woodman at Nuthampstead (off the A10, just south of Royston) where we had an excellent breakfast last year. The cost is expected to be £7 per head. As Alan and Barbara are on holiday at the moment, can anyone interested who hasn’t already put their name down, give me a ring.
 
Just a reminder of a few of the up and coming dates in the next few weeks. Chilterns Day at the Buckingham Railway Centre which is off the A41 just past Waddesdon is on July 2nd as is the car show at Fanshawes. The Hoddesdon Show is on Saturday 8th July and as usual Keith and Audrey have invited us to their house for a cuppa afterwards. The following Monday evening, 10th July, is when Dick has arranged for us to go to the SS meeting at Potters Crouch. Apparently, sandwiches and wine are being provided. Hopefully the Harpenden Show on 26th July will have better weather than last year. Don’t forget this is for pre-booked entrants only. We will be in the Stamford/Peterborough area over the last weekend in July and hopefully some of you will join us for XK Day on 30th July at Burghley House. All Jaguars and other makes of classic cars will be parked together. If it rains, Burghley House is well worth a visit, and the Orangery has food ranging from snacks to a full roast.
 
Brian K has given me the dates for this year’s shows at Croxley Green which I understand are like a mini Harpenden. The first is on Wednesday 2nd August (evening) and then on Sunday 17th September when the Carters’ Steam Fair will be there. (Great dodgems!)
 
That is some of what is to come, but looking back it has been a pretty awful start to the season. The JDC 50th at Eynsham Hall was held in constant pouring rain, with the grounds so bad in some places that the planned parking spaces for Areas such as ourselves couldn’t be used. It was a very disappointing day, with very little to see or do on such an awful day so most of us gave up by midday. At least we were able to do that but I felt sorry for people like Mick Duffy who were there to organise the event. There was a good turnout of cars despite the weather and I expect those who had come from various parts of the country and Europe for the ball on Saturday enjoyed the weekend. It has been several years since we have attended National Day as an Area and to be honest, unless there is more to do it is not likely to be a regular event for us.
 
The following weekend was even more of a let down as, for only the second time in its history, the Enfield Pageant had to be cancelled due to the dreadful condition of the grounds. Despite bringing in eight lorry loads of bark, it was still too muddy and first thing Sunday morning it had to be cancelled. The Clock House Nursery (thanks to Keith O’s son) had again provided us with some decorative planted pots and these have now been bought by some of our members with the money to go to the Little Haven Hospice, whose show we will be attending on 20th August. The amount of work that goes into putting on a show the size of the Pageant is enormous so it must be both frustrating and disappointing for those who work so hard before, during and afterwards.
 
At the end of June, Phil and Heather Kendon will be moving to Somerset, so we wish them well in their new home. For several years, Phil has been Club Archivist and Heather has always been a willing member of the bacon roll team, so thanks to them both for their help and support to the Area.
