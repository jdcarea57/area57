Newsletter - September 2004

For traditionalists the news that Jaguar production is to cease at Browns Lane is sad. I suppose, however, that it is inevitable that economics outweigh sentiment. It does seem strange though, that the traditional British Racing Green used in the company’s corporate identity is to change to black, with all the costs that this will involve. It is also being suggested that the "leaper" should be modernised.

TRENT PARK - August Bank Holiday Monday

Although the EDVVS used to hold shows at Trent Park quite a few years ago, this was the first time that they had been back since combining with the Heavy Horse Association. It’s a much more level site than Holly Hill Farm so it was easier to get the tent pegs in, especially as it was always rather stony there. As far as Area 57 goes it is the usual bacon roll start to the day, once the banner etc. is up. I can’t remember who first thought of doing our own bacon rolls several years ago, but it has proved to be a very popular idea.

The show was the usual mix of steam engines, cars, heavy horses and various stalls. Dave Norton did his usual good job of describing cars as they did a couple of circuits around the arena. He handed over commentary to a representative of the H.H.A., who explained the differences between the various types of heavy horse breeds and their harness and vehicles. The weather seemed to be under the impression that it was April as it alternated between brilliant sunshine and very heavy downpours, so our new tent was well and truly christened.

BUNTINGFORD - Saturday 4th September

This was the first time that Doug and I have been to this show, and we found it a pleasant relaxing day. Our XK joined the E-types of Alan, Len, Brian K., Brian F. and Ron. We managed to park almost together, just being split by an Allard saloon that we had seen at Trent Park, which went on to win the prize for the most unusual/interesting category. As is the case with Hoddesdon, there seems a different atmosphere at town centre shows. There was a varied selection of cars plus a few tractors and plenty of motor bikes. The organisers had managed to put together a special James Bond display, complete with the green XK8 from the latest Bond film and a Pierce Brosnan look-a-like. This was raising money for Cancer UK so they probably did quite well. I believe I am right in saying that the other prize winners were an XK150 DHC (most desirable) and an Austin 7 (best condition). Brilliant sunshine made the day and most of us sat on the shady side of the road. Needless to say, Len and Sally didn’t!

SEPTEMBER WEEKEND - NR TEWKESBURY

After a spell of lovely weather, the forecast for our weekend away at the Corse Lawn Hotel near Tewksbury was pretty gloomy to say the least. However, apart from an extremely heavy shower when we were nearly there, we were very lucky and had plenty of sunshine. We set off from Potters Bar with the Rogers and Oswicks, the latter in a borrowed X-type. We stopped for a very pleasant lunch at a pub between Aylesbury and Bicester. When we reached the hotel we found that Phil and Heather, Len and Sally and Keith and Audrey had beaten us by about half an hour. Although plenty of hotels have grounds, very few have gardens but here there were beds of dahlias everywhere. Our rooms, most named after local villages were very good, large and well appointed; real tea, not tea bags, cafetiere, fresh fruit and home made biscuits.

After an excellent dinner it was time to test the brains with al light hearted quiz and test the steady hands with Jenga (a tower of wooden bricks for those unfamiliar with the game). At breakfast on Saturday morning we all agreed to visit the Gloucestershire and Warwickshire Railway which started from a village about twenty minutes away. The other end of the line was at Cheltenham Race Course, ten miles away. Although they do hope eventually to extend to Stratford on Avon, at present the railway doesn’t enter Warwickshire at all. It does mean that they can call themselves the G.W.R. though. We broke our journey at Winchcombe and had a stroll down to the pretty little town about a mile from the station for a light lunch, before completing the journey. The dinner that night was just as good as Friday’s and then another quiz and more brain exercise in "Pass the Bomb", a game guaranteed to make even bookworms forget most words that they know.

All too soon it was Sunday morning and while the rest of us spent some time in Tewkesbury, Phil and Heather headed towards Bristol to their daughter’s, Len and Sally were off to Bicester Shopping Village for some retail therapy.

Although I put forward a selection of brochures for these weekends, I’m just as much in the dark as everyone else as to how satisfactory the hotel will turn out to be. It is very gratifying to find one as good as Corse Lawn.

A few of our cars were at a charity show at Fanshawes recently and also at Enfield Town Park, which I gather was closed for a while as an armed response unit had to deal with someone who was threatening his girlfriend with a chainsaw I know a few people also had a trip to Browns Lane which I am hoping may be reported on in the future.

AREA 16 DAY, SHENDISH MANOR - Sunday 22nd August

Report By Richard Rogers

Four Area 57 cars attended a very friendly show. The weather was good, and the food in the hotel was excellent. I received an award for pre 1960 cars, (there were only two, but it sounds good doesn’t it?). An enjoyable event. I will be going again next year.

SUMMER DROPHEAD DRIVE-IN, ACE CAFE - Tuesday 24th August

Report by Richard Rogers

Eric, myself and two friends took our XKs up the North Circular Road to Park Royal and the famous Ace Cafe. It is a very friendly place, with cars and motorbikes on show. We had a meal, and I have to say thet the traditional sausage and mash was very good. Another one to add to my list of return visits.
