The Enfield Pageant will be almost with us when you get this Newsletter. Hopefully the weather may have settled a bit. All the regulars know the procedure by now and what they need to bring. For our new members, please be on site by 9 a.m so that cars can be arranged to their best advantage and if you want to go walkabout early, leave your keys with someone in case your car needs to be moved. Tea, coffee and bacon rolls will all be provided but don’t forget to bring your own mug.

Eric has entry forms for the  show at Fanshawes near Hertford on 2nd July and Dick has started taking names for the show near Burnham on Crouch on August 20th in aid of Little Haven children’s hospice.

I have a form for the Rock ‘n’ Roll Day at Waltham Cross on Saturday 5th August, which is organised by Broxbourne Council. If anyone wants it or more information, just ask.

In the past Keith and Audrey have enjoyed several weekends away with the EDVVS and this year they were joined by Dick, Eileen, Eric and Margot. I gather they had mixed weather and although a visit to Ironbridge and lunch on the Severn Valley Railways were enjoyed, the hotel left a lot to be desired.

At the same time we should have been in N.Wales with the XK Register but with unlucky timing, Doug had to have his appendix out. Consequently, I don’t have a lot to write about this month, so I was very pleased to receive Albert’s write up of the London-Brighton Jaguar Run.

Last year, it was mentioned that we could visit the M.G. Club which meets every Monday at the Botany Bay Cricket Club off Enfield Ridgeway. I made contact with David Hill, their organiser, who said they would be very pleased to see us and could possibly organise a BBQ. We need to sort out a mutually convenient Monday for the lighter evenings.

Report by Albert Scales
Newsletter - May 2006
