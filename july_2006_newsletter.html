It seems ironic that in a year which saw the Pageant cancelled due to a fairly isolated wet spell, there has been plenty of sunshine ever since.

Roy and Kim’s eldest daughter, Emma, was the first Area 57 baby to be born after the formation of the club, and it was an indication of how the years have gone by, when Roy told us that she has just passed her driving test at the first attempt.

Some of you will know that for the past few months, Dick and Eric have been having a potato growing competition, leading to plenty of friendly rivalry not to say some cheating. The result is the occasion which Audrey has written about for me, (see below).

I have started a new Balmoral order, so if anyone is in need of sweatshirts, polo shirts etc. please let me know.

I am writing this on what is predicted to be a day of record breaking temperature and I am now going to mention Christmas. WHITEWEBBS MUSEUM PROJECT

This building conception has been talked about for many years at the EDVVS Club. Some people wanted a new clubhouse built to hold the meetings etc. with a fire station attached so the existing meeting room could be used as more Museum space. It was decided in the end to have a covered display area plus a fire station so the wheels were set in motion.

While some of you went to the SS Meeting at St. Albans this clashed with the meeting at the Museum where the committee updated the members on the progress so far.

Steve Brayshaw opened the meeting thanking Mike Pierce and Danny Smith for their previous input to this project. Then the meeting was handed over to Paul Wallace who has taken over the role of co-ordinating present and future works to the building, with Keith Childs, the full time worker at the Museum, as Project Manager, because he is on site every day to deal with day to day problems as they arise. Paul W. gave the background to the quotes and estimates that were required. Enfield Council planners finally agreed the type of brick we could use which just happened to be the one that was chosen a couple of years ago! They are hand made imperial Swanage Red sourced from E.H..Smiths of Bovingdon supplied by IDStock at a quoted price of £1400 a thousand, however, after a little bartering 20,000 bricks were supplied at a cost of £530 a thousand, so many thanks go to the people involved in that transaction. The building company we are now using is Oakland Refurbishing Ltd. of Stevenage who are making a very nice job of laying the bricks and blocks. They were helped, of course, by the good work done previously by Richard Rogers on footings and brickwork up to damp course level. The cost of brickworks above dampcourse is quoted as £26,860 labour only. To compliment the brickwork there will be a Portland stone porch and portico with signage to the fire station display area entrance, matching the front of the existing building, supplied and installed at the beginning of August at a cost of £26,000. There will also be a Victorian dial clock mounted above the stonework centre of the fire station entrance, which will be fitted with 14ft high wooden bi-fold doors still to be quoted for. At the moment the dial clock is being renovated by Phil Chard whose trade is clock repairs and who is also club treasurer. I believe the clock came from a building in the Winchmore Hill area. The side display area will have a stone portico with porchway to match the existing museum building, with up and over doors either side to maximise the access to the display area. The works are now proceeding at a steady rate with completion of building works due by the end of September. Total amount spent to date on this part of the project is £53,990 with an expected £68,000 to complete. A question from the floor of the meeting was about another floor to be added to fire station, but the answer was that this was not practical.  John Wilkinson had the tedious task of scaling down the area, the vehicles the club has to put in the new building, the requirements of a disabled person’s lift, fire lobby and exit would encroach on floor space so would not allow enough room for fire engine display etc. Plus the auto-jumble shop would have to be reduced by 6ft to allow for an external fire escape to be fitted. There were no more questions from the floor so Steve closed this part of the meeting asking members to show their appreciation to Paul Wallace and a number of members in the background who did not get a mention who have been constructive to this project.

If any member would like to view what’s going on with the building project etc. the museum is open every Tuesday 10 am - 4 pm or Paul W. said we can call in and speak to Keith Childs, John Wilkinson or Mick Barker during the week, the invitation is there so use it. EDVVS needs your continual support.

The Club has a few thousand programmes left over from the Enfield Pageant; it contains a calendar and adverts from companies and it was suggested that flyers for Trent Park Show and the Whitewebbs Museum were inserted and members were asked to take 25 copies to give to neighbours and friends to save wasting them.

SPUDS - U - JUDGE - Gala Event - Sunday 9th July 2006

By Audrey Williams

The morning started at 11 am at the first venue in Orchard Close, Cuffley. The Judge (KW) and participants (ET & RR) plus several witnesses took up their places on the grassy slopes of Eric’s garden. 5 bines were dug up and the potatoes were dutifully placed in the agreed receptacle. (Eric’s old black bucket!) It was noted this competitor had already been awarded a gold medal at Chelsea Flower Show for his giant (potato) specimen!! There was photographic evidence on display to prove it! After the ceremonial first dig celebratory champagne was served in the conservatory we then followed in convoy to Clay Hill allotments. Again the digging procedure was the same. Both potato harvests were transported by Keith O. to ensure no misdemeanor took place.

At Churchbury Road the specimens were duly weighed and measured. The judge awarded points up to a total of 30 - according to various categories and criteria. The marking sheets were filled in and it was announced Eric was the overall winner. We celebrated with a refreshing Pimms on the lawn. Certificates were presented to both parties with a comprehensive recipe book on potatoes to the winner.

Eileen as always provided refreshments whilst Margot recorded highlights of the event on video.

A thoroughly enjoyable event. Many thanks to everyone involved. Next year we hope to expand the event making it open to whoever may wish to enter.

It was suggested that we might make it into a sponsored competition and to give the proceeds to charity.

We look forward to "Spuds - U- Judge" 2007.
