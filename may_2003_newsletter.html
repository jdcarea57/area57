BREAKFAST RUN - 27th April 2003

Although some of our regulars weren’t able to make the the first Breakfast Run of the year, we still finished up with nearly forty people. The weather was chilly but dry; certainly not like last year when several peopled stayed on at Alan and Barbara Collins' place for an impromptu BBQ.

HARTHAM COMMON CENTENARY SHOW - 4th May 2003

This was an extremely successful show, which was marking the centenary of the then Mayor of Hertford’s invitation to members of the Automobile Club to drive their vehicles to Hertford in order to show the public the latest mode of transport. Almost two dozen pre First World War vehicles made an excellent display and were put through their paces around the arena during the afternoon. Eight of our cars had a perfect river-side position, where we were joined by several other Jaguars, a Swallow, and the Jaguar powered Allard Palm Beach which many of you have seen at various shows. A selection of our cars were asked to go around the arena before lining up for a chat with the commentator. Alan said a few words about our cars as they came in and, as a result, has asked all of us who take our cars to shows if they could let him have a brief account of their vehicles so that he can refer to them if the occasion arises. Dick Rogers took his Mk V, and as well as being part of our parade was also asked to show his car in the arena to represent the Fifties in a display by decade. There was a slight hiccup when a fuse blew, but everything was sorted out for his second appearance. There was a wide variety of cars, plus charity stalls and several new dealerships. The event was being run by five local Rotary Clubs and their marshalling and organisation was excellent. The glorious weather made this a very pleasant day out, which hopefully will have raised plenty of money for local charities as well.

XK WHITE ROSE WEEKEND 9-11th May 2003

We set off for this Yorkshire weekend with me nursing a rhododendron on my lap (Eileen Rogers' birthday present) and hoping that it wouldn’t lose too many petals before we gave it to her. The four of us set off in bright sunny weather, and all was well till just before Peterborough when Doug spotted Dick Rogers coasting to a halt on the hard shoulder. The problem turned out to be something not tightened enough on the distributor after a service a few days previously. Soon after that we replenished petrol tanks and stomachs and continued on our way. We arrived safely at our hotel looking out to sea between Scarborough and Whitby, after taking a few detours along some twisty hilly roads that we used to travel on when having several holidays in this area back in the seventies.

As usual on these weekends, the majority of people there were familiar faces. Saturday morning saw organizer Brian Davis give his usual welcome chat and point out a few things about the places we were to visit. The first stop was Eden Camp, originally built as a P.O.W. camp in 1942, it is now an excellent museum of civilian life during World War II, with each hut representing a particular theme. There is far too much to mention here as to do it justice takes several hours. One thing caused general amusement; an outside "lavvy" which on pushing the door regales you with "Hang on a minute!". The museum has won several well deserved Tourist Awards.

A totally different era for our next visit; Castle Howard which is where Brideshead Revisited was filmed. After a few problems, Brian eventually got agreement for us all to park on the grass in front to of the main facade of the house. It was a lovely setting with a large lake behind us. We made our own way around with guides in each room to answer questions. The grounds, needless to say were far too extensive to explore thoroughly. On returning to our cars, an American man was looking at our car and he called his elderly mother over saying that it was like the one she had bought for her husband years ago. He remembered crying when his father sold it and bought a Porsche. It must have been a DHC as he remembered being stuffed behind the seats.

The next morning we drove a few miles along the coast to the ruins of Whitby Abbey and its new visitor centre. Some people tackled the 199 steps down to Whitby town and back, which included resting places for coffins!

Back to the hotel for lunch and good-byes to those of the group who were heading for home. Like quite a few others, we had booked for an extra night and decided to drive back down to Whitby to see the town. It was here that the weather changed and it poured. Lots of surface water was on the road as we drove back to the hotel and an oncoming car drove through a puddle, most of which went over our windscreen and into our laps!

Luckily the weather was better the next morning and we only had a few showers on our way home. As usual Brian and Mary had organised a very enjoyable weekend and he is already planning next years, probably the South of England so we won’t have such a long journey as Plymouth last year and Yorkshire this time. We put nearly 650 miles on the clock.

A SOMERSET SOIREE - Friday May 2nd - Whitewebbs Museum weekend in Wells

Report by Audrey Williams

Murky, misty, wet & miserable -M25 Motorway madness.

A five hour journey in the E-type on a Friday afternoon was not the best start to the weekend! The Swan Hotel was a lovely and welcome surprise - an old 15th Century oak-beamed coaching inn, with views over the impressive cathedral. Beautiful surroundings and excellent food - plenty of roast potatoes! Parking, however at the back of the hotel for the 50 odd cars was a slight problem.

Saturday was mainly dry but cold. We drove in convoy to Wookey Hole for a guided tour of the caves & adjoining working paper mill. We both enjoyed a cream tea for our lunch (unusual) and Keith had a glass of the local scrumpy with his (even more unusual !)

The convoy moved on to Cheddar Gorge where an open-topped double-decker bus had been arranged to take us down into the village. We shivered on the top deck. In the evening, after another lovely meal, there was one of Whitewebbs’ now infamous raffles and a motor quiz which we may re-cycle at a later date.

The highlight of the weekend was a visit to the Haynes Motor Museum on Sunday morning. Amazing cars- but no photos of Wayne's wedding anywhere!! This was followed by lunch at a local inn and the presentation of the "piston broke" trophy and a "grope" photograph.

A great weekend and fantastic value - some friendly people (77 in all) and an open invitation for all members of the EDVVS in our club to join them on their next soiree in 2004.
